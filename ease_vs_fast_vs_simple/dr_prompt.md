# Deep Research Prompt

## Research Question
What is the complete distinction between the concepts of "ease", "fast", and "simple" in software engineering and product design — including their definitions, trade-offs, relationships, and practical implications?

## Purpose & Context
This research seeks a definitive framework distinguishing these three concepts which are frequently conflated. The core of the conversation is Rich Hickey's "Simple Made Easy" (2011) talk which distinguishes Simple (objective, not interleaved) vs Easy (subjective, familiar/near at hand). "Fast" appears in multiple senses: speed of development, speed of execution, speed of change. The audience needs a clear, sourced framework to use in engineering discussions.

## In Scope
1. Rich Hickey's "Simple Made Easy" framework — exact definitions, etymology, implications
2. The distinction between Simple (objective/structural) vs Easy (subjective/relative)
3. Where "Fast" fits: speed of development, speed of execution, speed of change, speed of understanding
4. The trade-off axes: Simple/Complex x Easy/Hard 2x2 matrix
5. Real-world implications: when to prioritize each, how they interact over time
6. Modern commentary (2020-2026) extending or critiquing Hickey's framework
7. Related frameworks: YAGNI vs SOLID as optimizing different axes, the "iron cross" of software trade-offs

## Required Dimensions
1. Definitions: Precise definitions of all three terms with etymology and formal distinctions
2. Objective vs Subjective: Why simple is objective and easy is subjective (and where fast sits)
3. Time dynamics: How easy gives early speed but creates long-term drag; simplicity requires upfront investment but sustains speed
4. The 2x2 matrix: Simple/Easy, Simple/Hard, Complex/Easy, Complex/Hard quadrants with examples
5. Organizational incentives: Why engineering environments reward easy over simple
6. Practical application: How to use this framework in real decision-making
7. Criticisms/limitations: Arguments against Hickey's framework (e.g., "Easy will always trump simple")

## Source Requirements
- Primary: Rich Hickey's "Simple Made Easy" talk transcript (2011) — this is the foundational source
- Supporting: Blog posts and articles engaging with/expanding the framework (2020-2026)
- At least 8-15 sources covering different angles
