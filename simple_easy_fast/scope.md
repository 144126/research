# Phase 1: SCOPE — Simple vs Easy vs Fast

## Research Question
What are the distinctions, relationships, and trade-offs between "ease" (easy to do/use), "fast" (quick to create/execute), and "simple" (low complexity, un-braided) in software engineering, product design, and project management?

## Core Components
1. **Simple**: Low complexity, single concern, un-braided (Hickey's definition). Objective property of interleaving.
2. **Easy**: Familiar, near to skill set, low cognitive load for a given user. Subjective.
3. **Fast**: Low latency to create/execute, high throughput. Temporal dimension.

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- Engineers: architectural trade-offs, technical debt
- Product managers: prioritization, feature velocity
- UX designers: learnability vs efficiency
- Executives: cost, speed to market, maintainability

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. Simple ≠ Easy (Hickey's core claim)
2. Fast trades off against both simple and easy in systematic ways
3. Technical debt is the measurable consequence of prioritizing speed
4. Context determines optimal trade-off (MVP vs enterprise vs safety-critical)
5. Simplicity-first yields sustainable long-term velocity
