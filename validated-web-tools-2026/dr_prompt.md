# Deep Research Prompt

## Research Question
What specific web tool or web app concept has the strongest validated market demand in 2026 — something people desperately need, would pay for immediately on release, and would go viral within hours of launch because everyone is talking about how useful it is?

## Purpose & Context
The requester is a builder/developer who wants to ship a web tool that has near-instant product-market fit. They want something with RED-HOT demand signals: people already complaining loudly about the problem, attempting broken workarounds, and explicitly saying "I wish this existed" or "take my money."

### Audience
- Primary: Solo developer/founder building a web tool they can ship fast
- Tone: Direct, evidence-driven, actionable
- Complexity: Technical enough for a developer but focused on market validation

### Decision Context
- The requester will pick ONE concept from this research to build next
- Stakes: Time investment of 2-8 weeks of full-time building

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. Web tools (browser-based applications, SaaS, browser extensions) only
2. Concepts with explicit demonstrated demand
3. Ideas a solo developer can build in ≤8 weeks
4. Monetization models that work for web tools
5. Virality mechanics

### Out of Scope
1. AI/ML research projects requiring custom model training
2. Marketplaces needing two-sided network effects from day one
3. Social networks
4. Hardware or native mobile apps
5. Enterprise-only B2B tools requiring sales teams

### Timeframe
Primary: 2025-2026. Secondary: 2020-2024 (historical context).

### Geographic Focus
Global, primarily English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia, EU).

## Required Dimensions
1. Technical/Mechanistic Exploration
2. Historical Context & Evolution
3. Current State-of-Art
4. Quantitative Evidence
5. Stakeholder Analysis
6. Competing Approaches Comparison
7. Criticisms & Limitations
8. Contrarian & Heterodox Perspectives
9. Future Trajectories & Predictions
10. Regulatory, Legal & Ethical Dimensions
11. Geographic & Geopolitical Variation
12. Practical Applications & Case Studies

## Seed Keywords
"I wish there was an app for" "why doesn't this exist" "take my money" "someone should build" "desperately need a tool" anti-cloud movement, local-first apps, privacy-first tools, subscription fatigue, burnout tracking, micro-SaaS architecture, solo developer tech stack

## Mode
Deep Research (8-phase pipeline, multi-level BFS expansion, 216+ sources target)
