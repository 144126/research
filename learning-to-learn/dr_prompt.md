# Deep Research Prompt

## Research Question
What does the cognitive science, educational psychology, and neuroscience research tell us about how to learn to learn — and how can a person (from student to lifelong learner) apply these findings to master new skills and knowledge faster, retain information longer, and develop genuine understanding?

## Purpose & Context
Produce a definitive, evidence-based guide on meta-learning — the skill of learning how to learn. Audience spans from students to professionals to educators. Topic has exploded in popularity (Barbara Oakley's "Learning How to Learn" MOOC, Scott Young's "Ultralearning"), yet most people still default to ineffective strategies like re-reading and highlighting.

### Audience
- **Primary:** 54-year-old man with deep academic exposure. Formal, precise language.
- **Secondary:** His 9-year-old child. Every hard concept gets inline ELI5 [imagine: ...] explanation.
- **Tone:** Precise, data-driven, rigorous, but warm enough that a child isn't alienated.

### Decision Context
- What concrete techniques should I adopt to learn faster and remember more?
- Which popular study methods are backed by evidence vs. myths?
- How should I structure a self-directed learning project?
- What role does technology (AI tools, spaced repetition apps) play?
- How important are mindset, sleep, and metacognition?

## Research Scope
In Scope: Evidence-based learning techniques, metacognition, neuroscience of learning, motivation/procrastination, ultralearning, Feynman Technique, debunked claims, tools/technology, domain variations, practical implementation.

Out of Scope: AI/ML meta-learning, corporate training, early childhood education, curriculum design, speed reading.

## Required Dimensions
1. Technical/Mechanistic: Core learning mechanisms
2. Historical Context & Evolution
3. Current State-of-Art: Most effective techniques
4. Quantitative Evidence: Effect sizes, meta-analyses
5. Stakeholder Analysis
6. Competing Approaches Comparison
7. Criticisms & Limitations
8. Contrarian & Heterodox Perspectives
9. Future Trajectories
10. Regulatory, Legal & Ethical Dimensions
11. Geographic & Geopolitical Variation
12. Practical Applications & Implementation
13. Public Opinion, User Sentiment & Social Proof

## Output Requirements
- index.md — Full markdown report
- evidence.jsonl — Claim-level evidence records
- sources.jsonl — Source registry
- run_manifest.json — Run metadata
- Minimum 270 sources, 3+ per major claim
- Inline [N] citations on every factual claim
- Every claim labeled with evidence class: 'expert/third-party', 'user-reported', 'vendor-sourced'
- ELI5 inline [imagine: ...] explanations
- Prose >=80%
- "Weakest Evidence" section at end
- Executive Summary >= 1000 words with citations

## Mode
Deep Research (8-phase pipeline, multi-level BFS expansion, 270+ sources)
