# Phase 1: SCOPE & Phase 2: PLAN

## Research Question
What does cognitive science, educational psychology, and neuroscience research tell us about how to learn to learn — and how can a person (from student to lifelong learner) apply these findings to master new skills and knowledge faster, retain information longer, and develop genuine understanding?

## Scope Boundaries

### In Scope
1. Evidence-based learning techniques (active recall, spaced repetition, interleaving, elaboration, dual coding)
2. Metacognition and self-regulated learning
3. Neuroscience of learning (memory consolidation, sleep, neuroplasticity)
4. Motivation, procrastination, and habit formation
5. Ultralearning and intensive self-directed learning
6. Feynman Technique and learning-by-teaching (protégé effect)
7. Debunked learning claims (learning styles, etc.)
8. Tools and technology (Anki, AI study assistants, SRS)
9. Domain variations (STEM, languages, humanities, motor skills)
10. Practical implementation: personal learning systems

### Out of Scope
- AI/ML meta-learning (machine learning subfield)
- Corporate training / organizational learning
- Early childhood education (focus: adolescent-to-adult)
- Specific curriculum design
- Speed reading claims

## Key Assumptions
1. Learning techniques generalize across populations (with individual differences noted)
2. Lab findings translate to real-world learning (ecological validity caveats noted)
3. Self-report data from forums provides valid experiential evidence
4. Meta-analytic findings represent best available evidence
5. The reader has basic familiarity with scientific concepts
