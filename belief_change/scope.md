# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Core Question Decomposition

1. **Mechanism**: What neural/cognitive processes underlie belief change?
2. **Technique comparison**: Which methods work best, fastest, most durably?
3. **Practical execution**: Exactly what steps must a person take?
4. **Evidence quality**: What is the strength of evidence for each approach?
5. **Individual differences**: Who responds to which technique?
6. **Risks/limitations**: When does belief change fail or backfire?

## Boundaries

**In:** SATS, self-hypnosis, clinical hypnosis, theta-state, subliminals, affirmations, self-persuasion, CBT, identity scripting, shadow work, neurofeedback, brainwave entrainment, "as if" principle

**Out:** Brainwashing, propaganda, religious conversion, psychedelic-assisted, philosophical approaches, commercial marketing

## Stakeholders
- Self-improvement practitioners
- Hypnotherapists/coaches
- Neuroscience researchers
- App developers
- Online communities (Reddit, forums)

## Success Criteria
- Identify the single most effective technique with supporting evidence
- Document exact step-by-step protocols
- Quantify effect sizes, time-to-change, durability
- Surface community-reported outcomes
- Acknowledge gaps and controversies honestly
