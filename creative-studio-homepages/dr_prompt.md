# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
How do professional creative studios and design agencies design, structure, and build their own homepages to attract premium clients — including every section, layout pattern, typography system, animation approach, technical stack decision, and messaging framework?

## Purpose & Context
This research is for a designer/developer who wants to build a homepage that competes with top creative studios (e.g., Pentagram, Clay, Obys, Lesse Studio, Sundown Studio, Properly Studio, Moah Studio). The goal is to understand not just surface-level visual trends but the strategic reasoning, structural patterns, technical implementation choices, and craft details that separate premium studio homepages from template-driven or amateur sites.

The audience needs actionable, code-backed patterns they can implement themselves, not just inspiration galleries.

### Audience
- **Primary**: Designers and frontend developers building a creative studio/agency homepage (intermediate to advanced skill level)
- **Secondary**: Agency founders evaluating their own website strategy
- **Tone**: Technical but accessible; precise about implementation; honest about trade-offs
- **Child-reader adaptivity**: Every jargon term and technical concept must include an inline ELI5 explanation

### Decision Context
- What decisions determine whether a studio homepage feels premium vs generic?
- Which layout patterns, animation techniques, and typography choices consistently appear on award-winning studio sites?
- What technical stacks do top studios choose and why?
- How do studios balance visual experimentation with clarity and conversion?
- What specific implementation patterns can be adopted directly?
