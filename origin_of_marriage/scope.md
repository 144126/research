# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Research Question
What is the exact, true origin of marriage as a human institution — cutting through mythology, religious tradition, and cultural bias to deliver the definitive evidence-based account of where, when, why, and how marriage first emerged?

## Core Components
1. Biological/evolutionary roots of pair-bonding in hominins
2. Hunter-gatherer ancestral marriage patterns (reconstructed phylogenetically)
3. First written evidence (Sumerian, Babylonian, Mesopotamian)
4. Neolithic Revolution transformation through agriculture and property
5. Economic function (brideprice, dowry, inheritance)
6. Religious institutionalization (Sumerian sacred marriage → Christian sacrament)
7. Legal evolution (Roman law → common law → civil marriage)
8. Romantic revolution (19th century love-based marriage)

## Scope Boundaries
- IN: Primate pair-bonding evolution, hominin mating systems, cross-cultural ethnographic data, ancient Near Eastern legal codes, Roman and English legal history, economic anthropology, religious history
- IN: Deep time (evolutionary) → modern civil marriage
- OUT: Specific modern marriage equality debates (same-sex marriage is beyond scope)
- OUT: Detailed treatment of non-Western marriage systems except as comparative data
- OUT: Marriage in specific contemporary religious traditions (e.g., Islamic nikah, Jewish ketubah) except where directly relevant to origins
- OUT: Wedding ceremonies, rituals, and celebrations (focus is on marriage as institution)

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources minimum
- 8 findings with 600-2000 words each
- Evidence class labels on every claim
- ELI5 inline explanations throughout
- Complete bibliography with no placeholders
- Weakest evidence self-audit section
- All 12 quality gates pass

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. Marriage is a cultural universal with deep evolutionary roots
2. The agricultural revolution was a major transformation point
3. Religious marriage is a late development, not original
4. Love-based marriage is historically very recent (19th century)
5. Brideprice/brideservice is ancestral to dowry
6. Monogamy was not the ancestral human condition
7. Hunter-gatherer marriage was primarily regulated and alliance-based
