# Phase 1: SCOPE - Research Framing

## Core Question
What is the current scientific and philosophical understanding of reflecting on one's own thoughts — encompassing metacognition, introspection, and self-reflection — including cognitive mechanisms, neural correlates, practical benefits, known limitations, and emerging applications in AI?

## Scope Boundaries
**In:** Cognitive/neural mechanisms of metacognition | Benefits for learning, clinical practice, well-being | Limitations and biases (introspection illusion) | Philosophical foundations | AI introspection research (2024-2026) | Metacognitive training interventions | Educational applications | Cultural variation

**Out:** Religious/mystical frameworks | Pre-1900 philosophy | Corporate/organizational reflective practice unrelated to cognition | Market research applications

## Key Assumptions
1. Metacognition is a measurable cognitive capacity with identifiable neural substrates
2. Introspective self-report is partially but not fully reliable
3. AI systems can exhibit forms of meta-cognition but not subjective introspection

## Stakeholders
Students, educators, clinicians, patients (esp. psychiatric), executives, athletes, AI/ML developers, neuroscientists, philosophers

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources across 5+ source types
- All 13 dimensions covered
- Evidence class labels on every claim
- ELI5 inline explanations throughout
- Weakest evidence self-audit section
- Both pro-self-reflection and skeptical/contrarian positions represented

# Phase 2: PLAN - Research Strategy

## Query Strategy (9 angles per iteration)
1. Core semantic: metacognition, introspection, self-reflection mechanisms
2. Technical: neural correlates, fMRI, EEG, computational models
3. Recent: 2024-2026 developments
4. Academic: papers, reviews, meta-analyses
5. Contrarian: introspection illusion, limits, skepticism
6. Statistical: effect sizes, prevalence, psychometrics
7. Industry/Applied: apps, training, education, therapy
8. Critical analysis: publication bias, measurement issues
9. Personal/user-reported: Reddit, forums, social media, reviews

## Knowledge Dependencies
1. Foundational theories (Flavell, Nelson & Narens, Koriat, Fleming)
2. Neural basis (PFC, DMN, ACC)
3. Measurement methods
4. Intervention effectiveness
5. Clinical deficits
6. AI introspection
7. Cultural variation
8. Limitations/controversies

## Source Targets
- Academic: 120+ (psychology, neuroscience, philosophy, education, AI)
- Industry/applied: 30+ (apps, training programs, education policy)
- News/popular science: 30+
- User-reported (Reddit, forums, social media, reviews): 60+
- Contrarian/critical: 30+
