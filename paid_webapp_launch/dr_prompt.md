# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What web applications had people paying for them immediately at launch (within days or weeks of going public), and what specific tactics, strategies, and conditions made that possible?

## Purpose & Context
This research aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of how solo founders and indie hackers achieve immediate launch revenue — the specific case studies, pre-launch strategies, pricing tactics, distribution methods, and audience-building approaches that enable a web app to generate paying customers from day one. The findings are intended for founders evaluating whether and how to structure their own launch for immediate revenue.

## Required Dimensions
1. Technical/Mechanistic: What infrastructure was required for immediate payment collection? How were MVPs structured?
2. Historical: How has the speed from idea to paying customer changed from 2023 to 2026?
3. Current State-of-Art: What are the most recent (2025-2026) case studies of immediate launch revenue?
4. Quantitative: What are the specific revenue numbers, conversion rates, and timeline metrics?
5. Stakeholders: Who are the key founders and what were their pre-existing audiences?
6. Competing Approaches: Pre-sell vs. build-first? Productized service vs. SaaS? Platform distribution vs. own channel?
7. Criticisms: What are the failure modes of immediate-revenue launches?
8. Contrarian: Is "build in public" actually replicable for zero-audience founders?
9. Future: How is AI-assisted development changing launch dynamics?
10. Practical: What are the specific, actionable playbooks for each founder profile?
