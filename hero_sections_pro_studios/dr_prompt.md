# Deep Research Prompt: Hero Sections by Professional Creative Studios

## Research Question
How do professional creative studios design and build awesome hero sections that convert, engage, and win awards? What are the complete technical, design, copywriting, animation, and performance strategies used by top-tier creative studios (e.g., Awwwards winners, award-winning agencies)?

## Report Requirements
- Full report with all 12 required sections
- 270+ minimum sources
- EVERY factual claim followed by [N] citation
- Evidence class labels: 'vendor-sourced', 'user-reported', 'expert/third-party'
- Inline ELI5 explanations [imagine: ...] for every hard concept
- Executive Summary >= 800 words with citations
- Prose >= 80%, bullets sparingly
- No placeholders, no vague attributions, no fabricated citations
- Speculative content labeled [SPECULATION]
- "Weakest Evidence" section at end listing 3 least-supported claims
- At least 4 source types represented including personal/user-reported sources
- Complete bibliography with every citation, no ranges
- At least one personal/user-reported source type required per section

1. Executive Summary (1000-1500 words)
2. Introduction (scope, methodology, assumptions, key terms with ELI5)
3. Main Analysis (6-8 findings, 600-2000 words each):
   - Finding 1: The Anatomy of a Pro-Grade Hero Section
   - Finding 2: 8 Hero Patterns and When to Use Each
   - Finding 3: Copywriting — The Commercial Engine
   - Finding 4: Animation & Motion Architecture
   - Finding 5: 3D, WebGL & Immersive Experiences

4. Synthesis & Insights

7. Weakest Evidence
