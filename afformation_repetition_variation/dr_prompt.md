# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
Should afformations (empowering questions used for mindset reprogramming) be practiced as a single repeated question (e.g., "Why am I so smart?" repeated verbatim every session) or as variations on a theme (e.g., rotating "Why am I so smart?", "Why am I so intelligent?", "Why do I always have awesome creative ideas")? What does evidence from neuroscience, cognitive psychology, learning theory, meditation research, and practitioner experience say about the optimal approach?

## Purpose & Context
This research builds on an existing report on the afformation technique (at ~/research/afformation_technique/report.md) which covered the six mechanisms of afformations but did not address this specific practice-design question. The user needs a definitive, evidence-informed answer on whether to use one question repeatedly or vary the phrasing.

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. Mechanism analysis: How does repetition of the SAME question vs VARIED questions differentially affect each of the 6 afformation mechanisms (RAS programming, DMN integration, Question-Behavior Effect, theta-state imprinting, presupposition bypassing, backfire avoidance)?
2. Neuroscience of repetition vs variety: Hebbian learning / long-term potentiation from repetition vs synaptic tagging / novelty processing from variation
3. Habituation effects: Does repeating the same question produce diminishing returns due to neural habituation/satiation?
4. Spacing/variety effect in learning research: Does the "varied practice > blocked practice" finding transfer to self-talk/belief reprogramming?
5. Meditation/mantra parallels: Single-mantra systems (TM) vs varied approaches in brain changes, engagement, and outcomes
6. Practitioner reports: Reddit (r/lawofattraction, r/NevilleGoddard, r/getdisciplined), Noah St. John's materials, TikTok
7. The Question-Behavior Effect literature: Single question per session or varied? Does novelty affect effect size?
8. ADHD/attention considerations: Which approach for shorter attention spans?
9. Long-term trajectory: Different optimal approaches for first 7 days vs months 2-6 vs beyond?
10. Transfer and generalization: Does variation promote broader belief change while repetition deepens specific belief change?
