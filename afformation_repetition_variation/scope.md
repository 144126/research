# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Research Question Decomposition

**Central Question:** Should afformations be practiced as a single repeated question or as variations on a theme?

### Core Components
1. **Mechanism Comparison:** How repetition vs variation differentially engage each of the 6 afformation mechanisms
2. **Neuroscience Foundation:** Hebbian learning (repetition) vs novelty processing (variation) — which produces stronger/sustainable belief change?
3. **Habituation Risk:** Does the brain habituate to repeated questions, causing diminishing returns?
4. **Learning Theory Transfer:** Does the well-established "varied practice > blocked practice" finding in motor/cognitive learning generalize to belief reprogramming?
5. **Meditation/Mantra Evidence:** What 50+ years of TM and mantra research reveal about repetition vs variation in consciousness change
6. **Practitioner Wisdom:** What Reddit, Noah St. John, TikTok, and app designers actually recommend and why
7. **QBE Literature:** Whether the question-behavior effect requires novelty or benefits from repetition
8. **Individual Differences:** ADHD, attention span, personality factors in optimal approach
9. **Temporal Trajectory:** Optimal approach for onboarding (days 1-7) vs maintenance (months 2-6) vs advanced practice
10. **Transfer vs Specificity:** Does variation generalize beliefs while repetition deepens specific beliefs?

## Scope Boundaries

### In Scope
- Afformation-specific mechanism analysis (RAS, DMN, QBE, theta-state, presupposition, backfire)
- Direct comparisons of single vs varied self-talk inquiry
- Repetition in mantra meditation (TM, Japa, bija) as analogical evidence
- Varied practice in learning research as analogical evidence
- Habituation/satiation in neural and behavioral contexts
- Practitioner reports from Reddit, forums, apps
- The QBE and self-prophecy literature
- ADHD and attention considerations
- Temporal progression (first 7 days to 6+ months)

### Out of Scope
- General efficacy of afformations vs other techniques (covered in previous report)
- Full review of all 6 mechanisms (only differential effects of repetition vs variation)
- Development of new afformation apps
- Clinical trial design
- Original empirical research

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. That learning theory's "varied practice > blocked practice" transfers to belief reprogramming
2. That mantra meditation findings generalize to afformation practice
3. That habituation occurs for self-directed questions as it does for external stimuli
4. That the QBE effect size is modulated by question novelty
5. That individual differences (ADHD, personality) meaningfully affect optimal approach

## Critical Concepts (with child explanations)
- **Hebbian learning:** [imagine: when two brain cells fire together, they make a stronger connection — like a path through a field that becomes clearer the more you walk it]
- **Habituation:** [imagine: the first time you hear a dripping faucet it's annoying, but after a while your brain stops noticing it]
- **Pattern separation:** [imagine: your brain has a filing system — varied questions create different files, single questions update the same file]
- **RAS (Reticular Activating System):** [imagine: a filter in your brain that decides what to show you; asking a question tells it "find this!"]
- **DMN (Default Mode Network):** [imagine: the daydreaming network in your brain that activates when you're not focused on a task]
- **Theta-state:** [imagine: the brainwave state right before sleep — very relaxed, very open to suggestions]
- **QBE (Question-Behavior Effect):** [imagine: just asking someone if they'll exercise makes them more likely to exercise — weird but true]
- **Presupposition:** [imagine: a hidden assumption in a question — "why am I so smart?" assumes you ARE smart, and your brain goes along with it]
- **Contextual interference:** [imagine: learning by mixing things up is harder in the moment but creates stronger memory later]
