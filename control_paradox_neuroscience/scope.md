# Scope Document: Control Paradox Neuroscience

## Research Question Decomposition
1. What neural circuits mediate the transition from adaptive control to maladaptive overcontrol?
2. How does hypervigilance alter PFC-amygdala coupling?
3. What neurocomputational mechanisms explain why effort backfires (Costanzo 2025)?
4. How does the DMN facilitate or resist surrendering control?
5. What dopaminergic pathways govern effort valuation in overcontrol?
6. Which neural changes accompany acceptance-based interventions?

## Boundaries
**IN:** PFC-amygdala circuitry; ACC error monitoring; insula/interoception; striatal dopamine effort valuation; DMN in rumination vs acceptance; stress neurobiology (HPA axis, cortisol); neurocomputational models of metacontrol (Sandbrink 2026, Costanzo 2025); sense of agency neuroscience; predictive coding accounts; neural correlates of psychological flexibility; neuroimaging of acceptance vs suppression; RO-DBT neural basis; meditation neuroscience (DMN changes)

**OUT:** Clinical treatment protocols; cultural/historical/philosophical dimensions; organizational behavior; sports psychology in isolation; detailed drug mechanisms

## Key Assumptions
1. "Overcontrol" is a neurobiologically distinct state, not merely a personality trait
2. fMRI can distinguish control strategies at sufficient resolution
3. Rodent models of effort-based choice translate to human metacontrol
4. The control paradox is universal across individuals (varying only in threshold)
5. Neuroplastic changes underlie acceptance-based intervention efficacy
