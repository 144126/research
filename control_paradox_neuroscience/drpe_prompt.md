## Research Question
What are the neural mechanisms underlying the control paradox — the phenomenon where excessive cognitive control, hypervigilance, and over-monitoring of outcomes degrades performance, well-being, and self-regulation — and what neurobiological pathways explain why relinquishing control (via psychological flexibility, acceptance, or surrender) restores optimal function?

## Audience & Purpose
A 54-year-old man with deep academic exposure, wanting findings also accessible to a 9-year-old (inline ELI5 explanations). The question arises from personal experience: excessive effort backfires. This time the focus is specifically on the brain mechanisms. Informs personal philosophy and understanding of self-regulation.
