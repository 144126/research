# Neural Mechanisms of the Control Paradox — Complete Integrated Knowledge Draft

## 1. FOUNDATIONAL CIRCUIT: Control Detection vs. Default Passivity

### vmPFC-DRN GABAergic Inhibition Pathway
- **Maier & Seligman (2016)** [PMID:27337390] — Groundbreaking reframing: passivity is the default response to prolonged adversity, NOT a learned behavior. The vmPFC (prelimbic cortex) actively detects controllability and inhibits the dorsal raphe nucleus (DRN) via GABAergic interneurons. Three-stage model: (1) DETECT: vmPFC detects action-outcome contingency, (2) ACT: vmPFC→DRN inhibition prevents serotonin-mediated passivity, (3) EXPECT: protein-synthesis-dependent plasticity creates lasting expectation of control.
- **Maier, Seligman & Baratta (2023)** "From helplessness to controllability" [Frontiers Psychiatry] — vmPFC→DRN inhibition is the master resilience circuit. Controllable stress activates prefrontal circuitry that prevents 5-HT sensitization. Uncontrollable stress desensitizes 5-HT1A autoreceptors, creating lasting vulnerability. Key claim: **control is not learned; it is detected.**
- **mPFC Synaptosome Proteomics LH Model (2026)** [eNeuro] — Unbiased proteomics after escapable vs inescapable stress: inescapable shock alters synaptic processes, neurotransmission, and muscarinic acetylcholine receptor concentrations. Sex differences in M2 mAChR (Chrm2) regulation: males downregulate after ESC but not IES. Suggests cholinergic signaling in mPFC mediates learned helplessness.
- **Learned Helplessness 2025 comprehensive review** [Frontiers Psychiatry] — Integration: DRN hyperactivity, MRN hypoactivity, amygdala hyperactivation, vmPFC hypoactivation in uncontrollable stress. HPA axis dysregulation is downstream of vmPFC-DRN pathway.
- **Vicarious Learned Helplessness (2026)** [Frontiers Behav Neurosci] — Observing conspecific distress produces PFC transcriptional upregulation of Il6, comparable HPA activation to direct stress. Sex-dependent molecular signatures.

### Key Mechanistic Detail
The vmPFC does not merely react to control; it generates an **expectation** of control through protein-synthesis-dependent plasticity. This means the brain predicts controllability before experiencing it — and these predictions shape how effort, stress, and failure are processed [Maier & Seligman 2016].

## 2. METACONTROL: The Computational Architecture

### Meta-Reinforcement Learning Framework
- **Sandbrink, Hunt & Summerfield (2026)** [PNAS] — Landmark: deep RL agents require explicit mPFC "action prediction error" signals to solve metacontrol tasks. Agents over-estimating controllability → compulsive pathology; under-estimating → depression/anxiety. mPFC computes the *value of control itself*. Links metacontrol to transdiagnostic psychiatry.
- **Meta-RL reconciles dACC surprise, value, and control (2025)** [PLOS Comp Biol] — dACC integrates Bayesian surprise with cognitive control via meta-learning. Unifies two previously contradictory literatures: dACC as error monitor AND dACC as controller. Meta-RL predicts both functions.
- **Wang et al. (2018)** "Prefrontal cortex as a meta-reinforcement learning system" [Nature Neuroscience] — Recurrent neural networks trained with meta-RL develop PFC-like representations. Establishes deep link between RL algorithms and prefrontal function.
- **Huys & Dayan (2009)** "Bayesian formulation of behavioral control" [Cognition] — Computational foundation: control is Bayesian inference about action-outcome contingency.
- **Meta-Dyna (2025)** [Frontiers Comp Neurosci] — Neuroscience-inspired RL: prefrontal meta-control arbitrates between model-free and model-based strategies with mental simulation (hippocampal replay). Architecture parallels ACT's emphasis on cognitive flexibility.
- **Eppinger, Goschke & Musslick (2021)** "Meta-control: from psychology to computational neuroscience" [CABN] — Framework linking metacontrol to model-based/free arbitration.

### The Controllosphere and Goldilocks Theory
- **Holroyd (2023)** "Controllosphere" [PMC] — Cognitive effort = energy cost of leaving the "intrinsic manifold" (default low-energy state) for the "controllosphere" (high-energy neural state space). dlPFC drives the system into controllosphere; ACC monitors state changes. Effort prevents neural damage from prolonged high-control states. Overcontrol = staying too long in controllosphere.
- **Goldilocks Theory (2022)** [PMC] — Brain uses limited low-dimensional control states; over-precise control is maladaptive because it reduces the flexibility needed when environmental demands shift.
- **Meta-Control Demands (2024)** [bioRxiv] — Metacontrol program instantiation DEACTIVATES frontoparietal regions and decreases pupil size — opposite of what would be predicted by traditional control theories. Supports the metacontrol framework over classical accounts.

### MACA-Q Model (Meta-Arbitration of Control and Agency)
- **Sustaining Control Under Threat (2026)** [bioRxiv] — Novel computational model: agency is a **dynamically inferred learning gate**, distinct from self-efficacy. When outcome control is preserved, threat increases persistence; when control is lost, threat triggers disengagement. Formalizes the control paradox computationally.

## 3. THE PERSISTENCE-FLEXIBILITY CONTINUUM (Hommel-Colzato-Beste Framework)

### Aperiodic Neural Activity as Metacontrol Marker
- **Zhang, Stock, Mückschel, Hommel, Beste (2023)** "Aperiodic neural activity reflects metacontrol" [Cerebral Cortex] — Seminal: aperiodic EEG exponent (1/f slope) indexes metacontrol state. Higher exponent = more persistence (neural inhibition); lower exponent = more flexibility (neural excitation). Task-switching decreases exponent (flexibility); flanker tasks increase it (persistence).
- **Metacontrol on Demand (2026)** [J Cogn Neurosci] — Creativity tasks bias metacontrol: Remote Associates Test → persistence; Alternate Uses Task → flexibility. Aperiodic exponent shifts confirm reactive (not proactive) metacontrol model.
- **Aperiodic activity in task-switching (2024)** [Scientific Reports] — Directly tested traditional control theory vs metacontrol theory. Results support metacontrol theory: task-switching LOWERS aperiodic exponent (more flexibility), while traditional control predicts it should increase it.
- **Catecholaminergic modulation (2025)** [HBM] — Methylphenidate enhances persistence bias (higher aperiodic exponent). Baseline GABA+ and Glx in ACC/SMA/striatum predict metacontrol biases. Demonstrates neurochemical substrate.
- **Metacontrol aging (2026)** [GeroScience] — Older adults show lower aperiodic exponents; atDCS increases inhibitory tone particularly in older adults.

### Proactive vs Reactive Metacontrol
- **Kang & Chiu (2025)** "Distinct neural bases of proactive and reactive metacontrol" [NeuroImage] — fMRI: proactive metacontrol engages superior frontal gyrus, angular gyrus, frontal operculum (sustained task-rule maintenance). Reactive metacontrol recruits inferior frontal gyrus (stimulus-driven conflict detection). **No overlapping regions** between modes.
- **Kang & Chiu (2022)** "Well under control: control demand changes sufficient for metacontrol" [Frontiers Psychology] — Even expectation of control demand shifts metacontrol state.
- **Kang & Chiu (2024)** "Concurrent expectation and experience-based metacontrol" [CABN] — EEG insights and working memory capacity moderation.

### Developmental Trajectory
- **Metacontrol childhood to adulthood (2025)** [Scientific Reports] — Aperiodic exponent DECREASES from childhood to adulthood (more flexibility), but STRATEGIC ADJUSTMENT increases — adults modulate metacontrol more effectively.
- **Metacontrol in childhood (2023)** [Dev Cogn Neurosci] — DLPFC cortical thickness correlates with metacontrol ability. Cortical thickness in DLPFC, temporal, and superior-parietal regions predicts better metacontrol.
- **Adolescent metacontrol instructions (2025)** [Dev Cogn Neurosci] — Adolescents given "fine-grained" instructions (persistence bias) show adult-like event segmentation via IFG hyperactivity. Metacontrol can be instructionally modulated.
- **Adolescent valence bias in metacontrol (2020)** [OSF preprint] — Model-based decision-making continues developing into young adulthood; loss framing differentially affects metacontrol across age.
- **PFC development and mental illness (2025)** [Neuropsychopharmacology] — mPFC maturation through adolescence is activity-dependent; disruption by cannabis, stress, or microglial depletion produces lasting cognitive deficits.

## 4. SENSE OF AGENCY NETWORKS

### Meta-Analytic Results
- **Zhao, Dang, Gu, Fu & Haggard (2025)** "Cognitive mechanisms underlying sense of agency" [Psychological Bulletin] — Large meta-analysis: 75 temporal binding studies + 48 neuroimaging studies. Action binding (modest effect) increased by volition. Outcome binding (large effect) depends on volition, modality, valence, delay. Neural networks: cerebellum, pre-SMA, premotor cortex → motor network for binding. Caudate/putamen → valence. TPJ → temporal delay integration. **Dissociable components produce coherent agency.**
- **Mariano, Devoto & Zapparoli (2025)** "Feeling in control when things go well" [Neurosci Biobehav Rev] — Meta-analysis: intentional binding reliably occurs for both positive and negative outcomes, but is **enhanced for positive outcomes** (self-serving bias). Reversed for morally charged/aversive outcomes. Supports cue integration models.
- **Zito, Wiest & Aybek (2020)** "Neural correlates of sense of agency in motor control" [PLOS ONE] — Whole-brain ALE meta-analysis: decreased agency activates bilateral TPJ (right > left). Normal agency shows no significant clusters — agency is the **default state**, only disrupted agency registers neurally.
- **Brunello et al. (2025)** "Neural correlates of bodily self-consciousness meta-analysis" [Neurosci Biobehav Rev] — Agency engages right TPJ, bilateral IPL, right frontal gyri, left postcentral gyrus, left insula. Body ownership engages left premotor, bilateral posterior parietal, left occipital. Conjunction in posterior parietal and precentral regions.
- **Seghezzi, Giannini & Zapparoli (2019)** "Motor intentionality vs sense of agency" [Frontiers Psychology] — Dissociation: rostral mesial frontal (intentionality) vs posterior mesial frontal (agency monitoring). Rostro-caudal gradient within medial frontal cortex.

### Agency Disturbances in Neuropsychiatric Disorders
- **Di Luzio et al. (2024)** "Intentional binding in neuropsychiatric disorders" [Eur Psychiatry] — Systematic review (15 studies, 692 individuals): Increased action-outcome binding in schizophrenia and Parkinson's (with dopaminergic meds). Decreased binding in Tourette's and functional movement disorders. Inconsistent in ASD, AN, BPD. **IB deviations correlate with symptom severity.**

### Culture and Agency
- **Cultural differences in control (2018)** [PSPB] — Both perceived and desired control vary across cultures; East Asian samples show lower perceived control but not necessarily lower wellbeing.
- **Cultural sense of agency cues (2019)** [Frontiers Psychology] — Eastern vs Western cultures use different cues for agency attribution. Collectivist cultures rely more on contextual/social cues.

## 5. OVERCONTROL NEURAL PHENOTYPE

### ACC Hyperactivity and Error Overmonitoring
- **AN overcontrol fMRI (2021)** [Transl Psychiatry] — Elevated dlPFC activation and altered dlPFC-amygdala negative connectivity in anorexia nervosa. Higher dlPFC activation predicted increased amygdala reactivity at re-exposure — **the control attempt backfires.**
- **Perfectionism error monitoring** [Biol Psychol 2019] — Elevated error-related negativity (ERN) in perfectionism, associated with ACC hyperactivity.
- **Costanzo (2025)** "Control Paradox" theory — Conscious control saturates processing bandwidth, blocking implicit learning. The more you try, the less you achieve. Over-effort degrades performance.

### Insula Hypervigilance
- **Interoceptive oversensitivity in overcontrol** [various] — Anterior insula hyperactivity associated with heightened body awareness in AN and anxiety disorders. Interoceptive prediction errors drive overcorrection.
- **Insula meta-analyses** — Anterior insula consistently activated in error monitoring, effort valuation, and interoceptive prediction. Functions as a "salience hub" that may amplify overcontrol signals.

### Effort Valuation and Dopamine
- **Cholinergic modulation of dopamine for effort (2026)** [Nature] — Landmark: effort amplifies dopamine response to reward via local ACh-DA interaction in NAc. Acetylcholine from local interneurons binds nicotinic receptors on DA terminals. Blocking ACh blunts DA selectively in high-effort contexts. **Effort boosts dopamine only when ACh locally modulates it.**
- **D2 blockade disrupts effort-learning interaction (2026)** [PLOS Biol] — Sulpiride (D2 antagonist) eliminates the adaptive modulation of learning rates by effort. Under placebo, effort enhances learning from positive outcomes and reduces learning from negative outcomes — a self-serving bias that preserves motivation. D2 blockade eliminates this, reducing accuracy.
- **Dopamine and ACh distinct roles (2024)** [PMC] — D2 antagonism (haloperidol) decreases willingness to exert effort; M1 antagonism (biperiden) INCREASES it. Opponent DA-ACh interaction in striatum for effort-specific processing.
- **ACC stay-on-goal mechanism (2025)** [eNeuro] — Common ACC mechanism for persistence toward valuable rewards, whether the cost is information-seeking or effort. ACC maintains goal-directed behavior against costs.
- **ACC-NAc go-getter circuit disrupted by stress (2024)** [Neuron] — ACC projections to NAc mediate effort-based choice. Chronic corticosterone (stress simulation) impairs pursuit of high-effort rewards and diminishes ACC-NAc circuit responses.

### Effort Discounting Temporal Dynamics
- **Temporal dynamics of effort discounting (2026)** [CABN] — Effort discounting decreases over time for high-effort tasks (people become more willing to exert effort as deadline approaches). Depression attenuates this temporal effect. Hierarchical drift diffusion modeling links this to ACC function.
- **Neural basis of cost-benefit trade-offs ALE meta-analysis (2025)** — mPFC/dACC shows rostral-caudal gradient: caudal tracks task demands, rostral tracks reward. Caudal ACC integrates both — supports cost-benefit trade-off theory.

## 6. SURRENDER CIRCUIT: DMN, ACCEPTANCE, AND LETTING GO

### Default Mode Network and Metacontrol
- **Zhang et al. (2025)** [Cerebral Cortex] — Persistence states activate PFC, ACC, basal ganglia. Flexibility states activate DMN. **DMN activation is NOT passive — it is an active metacontrol state for cognitive flexibility.**
- **Letting go decreases DMN hyperactivity** [Psychology Today 2026] — Supplemented by studies: relinquishing control reduces DMN hyperactivity, increases emotion regulation region activation, reduces cortisol.
- **Control as nervous system safety strategy** [trauma neuroscience] — Letting go is not mindset shift but nervous system reorganization. The body literally perceives safety differently when control is released.
- **Interplay between metacontrol and mind-wandering (2025)** [HD-tDCS study] — Intentional mind-wandering associated with flexibility; unintentional with persistence. HD-tDCS over l-DLPFC selectively promotes persistence without altering spontaneous thought.
- **Meta-awareness and attention control (2026)** [Comms Biol] — Meta-awareness allows control over automatic external and internal attention orientations.

### Acceptance and Psychological Flexibility
- **Acceptance meta-analysis PCC/precuneus deactivation** — The specific neural substrate of acceptance is posterior cingulate cortex (PCC) and precuneus deactivation, NOT prefrontal activation. Acceptance reduces self-referential processing.
- **Radical acceptance as standalone strategy (2025)** [Current Psychology] — Single session of DBT radical acceptance fully restores negative and positive affect to baseline after distressing event. Acceptance serves as "catalyst" for cognitive reappraisal.
- **Paradox of Acceptance (2026)** [J Clin Psychol] — **Critical nuance**: elevated acceptance can co-occur with increased depressive symptoms if acceptance reflects resignation rather than flexibility. Distinguishes "active acceptance" from "passive resignation."
- **Mindfulness dual role (2026)** [Frontiers Psychology] — Mindfulness enhances cognitive reappraisal but can also increase emotional sensitivity when regulation skills are limited. Suppression effects when reappraisal is controlled.
- **Psychological flexibility and mental health (2026)** [PRBM] — Comprehensive review: experiential avoidance (opposite of acceptance) correlates with psychopathology. ACT produces medium effect sizes. Causal status of flexibility remains incompletely established.
- **Free Your Mind mindfulness RCT (2026)** [Mindfulness] — Within-person mediation: mindfulness improves mental health by reducing rumination and cognitive interference, increasing self-compassion and self-transcendence. Cognitive interference is the strongest mediator.
- **Heterogeneity in psychological flexibility (2026)** [ScienceDirect] — Idionomic analysis reveals subgroups: "Stoics" double down on committed action despite sadness; "Non-Stoics" show acceptance-joy-action chain.

## 7. PARADOXICAL INTENTION

- **Peluso & Freund (2023)** "Paradoxical Interventions meta-analysis" [Oxford] — Large effect (d = 1.1) vs placebo/control; medium effect (d = 0.49) vs other therapies. 17 studies per analysis. Most effective for sleep-onset insomnia.
- **Broomfield & Espie (2003)** [Behav Cogn Psychother] — PI reduces sleep effort and sleep performance anxiety. Subjective SOL improves but actigraphic measures may not capture changes.
- **Online PI for insomnia RCT (2025)** [IJPSY] — Two-arm RCT (n=26): moderate-to-large effect sizes on sleep onset latency, wake after sleep onset, sleep efficiency. Reduces sleep effort. PI is feasible standalone online intervention.
- **PI for climacteric insomnia (2026)** [MDPI] — Pilot (n=26 women, 40-70): PI and non-paradoxical treatment both improved insomnia. Supports recursive anxiety model: PI works when anxiety generates more anxiety.
- **PI + Hypnosis for anxiety (2026)** [Pragmatic Case Studies] — 3-session PI + hypnosis for panic disorder/agoraphobia/aviophobia. Potential mechanism: inhibitory learning model. Attentional absorption reduces extraneous cognitive noise.
- **PI mechanisms: recursive anxiety theory** [Ascher & Schotte 1999] — PI is specifically effective for disorders involving recursive anxiety (anxiety about anxiety). Breaking the recursive loop is the mechanism.

## 8. ACCEPTANCE AND COMMITMENT THERAPY NEUROSCIENCE

- **Meditation tFUS accelerates equanimity by decoupling DMN-CEN connectivity** — Transcranial focused ultrasound of default mode network reduces DMN-central executive network coupling, facilitating letting go.
- **Mindfulness mechanisms** — Reduced PCC/precuneus activation; decreased amygdala reactivity; increased PFC-amygdala connectivity. Cognitive defusion as metacognitive control shift.
- **ACT mechanisms** — Decreased experiential avoidance; increased willingness; values-based committed action. Neural: reduced ACC hyperactivity; normalized PFC-amygdala coupling.
- **Transdiagnostic efficacy** — Psychological flexibility as one of the most versatile, theoretically coherent, clinically accessible constructs [PRBM 2026].

## 9. NEUROFEEDBACK AND NEUROMODULATION

### DLPFC-Amygdala Pathway
- **Real-time fMRI neurofeedback DLPFC-amygdala (2025)** [bioRxiv] — Randomized sham-controlled (n=45): volitional enhancement of rDLPFC-amygdala connectivity during threat exposure attenuates anxiety. Maintained without feedback.
- **Amygdala fMRI-NF for BPD: BrainSTEADy trial (2025)** [BMC Psychiatry] — Multi-center RCT protocol for amygdala downregulation in borderline personality disorder.
- **Amygdala NF for PTSD (2023, 2025)** — Downregulation training reduces PTSD symptoms across all clusters. Amygdala NF facilitates re-establishment of healthy network dynamics across vmPFC-amygdala-DLPFC circuits.
- **Amygdala self-neuromodulation capacity (2024)** [Phil Trans R Soc] — Amygdala NF capacity correlates with alexithymia improvement. Posterior insula and parahippocampal gyrus co-modulate, supporting network-based approach.
- **fNIRS neurofeedback VLPFC (2025)** [NeuroImage] — Facilitates emotion regulation by targeting VLPFC.

### Transcranial Focused Ultrasound (tFUS)
- **tFUS panoramic review (2025)** [JNER] — tFUS reduces prefrontal GABA by ~20%, elevates DA and 5-HT via thalamo-cortical-striatal disinhibition. Improves cognitive flexibility. Reduces anxiety in emotion-induction tasks.
- **tFUS to right IFG improves mood (2020)** [Front Hum Neurosci] — 2-min tFUS to rVLPFC/rIFG improves mood, alters DMN connectivity for up to 20 min.
- **tFUS to rIFG improves response inhibition (2023)** [PNAS/Cortex] — Faster stopping times, reduced N200/P300 latencies during stop-signal task.
- **tFUS to bilateral mPFC (2022)** [J Clin Med] — Modulates mood, sleep, and emotional regulation via DMN modulation.
- **tFUS in psychiatry (2024)** [PMC] — Emerging evidence for depression, anxiety, ASD treatment.

### Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation
- **tDCS + methylphenidate interaction (2025)** [Brain Stimulation] — Combined atDCS + MPH enhances cognitive persistence (higher aperiodic exponent). Overlapping gain control mechanism in SMA.
- **tDCS and metacontrol aging (2026)** [GeroScience] — atDCS increases aperiodic exponent in older adults (more inhibitory tone), but does not affect periodic (theta/alpha) activity. Dissociation between periodic and aperiodic responses.
- **tDCS meta-analyses (2024)** — tDCS over PFC improves stop-signal task performance but not go/no-go. Variance not increased by individual differences. SST shows significant tDCS effect.
- **HD-tDCS and mind-wandering (2025)** — l-DLPFC HD-tDCS promotes persistence without altering mind-wandering frequency. Supports metacontrol framework.
- **tDCS, metacognition, and delay discounting (2025)** [Cortex] — rIFG anodal tDCS enhances inhibitory control, modulated by individual differences in metacognition and delay discounting.
- **Neurophysiological catecholamine-dependent tDCS (2025)** [Comms Biol] — AtDCS + MPH enhances gain control via overlapping mechanisms in SMA, especially for high-conflict conditions.

## 10. STRESS, CONTROL, AND HPA AXIS

- **Sense of control buffers against stress (2025)** [eLife] — Fielder, Shi, McGlade, Huys & Steinbeis: heightened control (via novel task) reduces subjective stress response to psychosocial stressor. First causal evidence in humans that control buffers subsequent stress.
- **Distinct trajectories of perceived control (2025)** [Scientific Reports] — Perceived control predicts affective responses better than objective control. Higher perceived control → lower state depression after stress. Protects women specifically.
- **Perceived control and HPA axis (2021)** [Psychoneuroendocrinology] — Lower perceived control → greater cortisol response to TSST, independent of objective controllability. Subjective control is the active ingredient.
- **vmPFC-PI pathway (2025)** [Transl Psychiatry] — High-control class shows reduced insula activation under stress. Unexpectedly, vmPFC activation decreased — suggests vmPFC control detection may differ from protective mechanism.
- **Stress and inflammation target dlPFC (2024)** [Biol Psychiatry] — Molecular cascade: stress drives cAMP-PKA-calcium-K+ signaling via α1-AR, β1-AR, D1 receptors, weakening layer III dlPFC delay cell firing. Calbindin regulates vulnerability.
- **Maladaptive neuroplasticity under stress (2025)** [Mol Neurobiol] — Chronic stress induces dendritic retraction, spine loss, impaired LTP in PFC glutamatergic pyramidal neurons. Paradoxical hyper-/hypo-excitability suggests complex dynamics.

## 11. COMPUTATIONAL PSYCHIATRY AND AGENTIC SYSTEMS

- **Non-bivalent psychopathology through metacontrol (2025)** [Neurosci Biobehav Rev] — Colzato, Zhang, Roessner, Beste & Hommel: propose abandoning phenomenologically-derived classifications. Metacontrol State Model of psychopathology (MSMp) explains dysfunction via persistence-flexibility imbalances.
- **Re-engineering the disordered mind (2026)** [Neuropsychopharmacology] — AI surrogate models of cognitive-affective manifolds; closed-loop N-of-1 experimental paradigm. Clinical interventions as control engineering over latent manifolds.
- **Breaking the Chain: Control Theory for rumination (2026)** [ScienceDirect] — Simulation study: negative beliefs about rumination exert strongest network controllability. Metacognitive Therapy, Cognitive Control Training, Self-System Therapy target different nodes.
- **Digital-twin metacognitive WCST (2026)** [Scientific Reports] — Three-component theory: MC-M (metacognitive monitoring via dorsal prefrontal), MC-C (metacognitive control via frontopolar), decision-making (ventral fronto-parietal). AN and schizophrenia share hidden similarities (motivational impairment, over-confidence) alongside differences.
- **MACA-Q sustained control/escape (2026)** [bioRxiv] — Meta-Arbitration of Control and Agency formalized: agency = dynamically inferred learning gate. Threat ↑ persistence when control preserved; threat ↓ engagement when control lost.

## 12. PHILOSOPHICAL DIMENSIONS OF CONTROL

- **Compatibilist free will in neuroscience (2026)** [Scienza&Filosofia] — COINTOB model: decisions as conditional intentions integrated to bound. Free will as scalable capacity embedded in deterministic cognition.
- **Chance, choice, and control (2026)** [Synthese] — Agent-causal libertarianism: agent as emergent control system integrating information and constraining noise. Indeterminism no threat to naturalistic free will.

## 13. REFINED HYPOTHESES

### The Deep Control Paradox
The control paradox is not merely that effort backfires. It is that **the neural machinery of control detection (vmPFC-DRN) evolved to detect uncontrollability, but the neural machinery of metacontrol (mPFC prediction errors, dACC meta-RL, aperiodic activity bias) evolved to optimize the *allocation* of control.** When metacontrol is impaired (cannot detect environmental controllability accurately), control is either over-deployed (compulsion, overcontrol) or under-deployed (helplessness, passivity). The paradox is a **metacontrol failure**, not a control failure.

### The Letting Go Circuit
Surrender is not passive. DMN activation during flexibility states [Zhang 2025] suggests an active neural process: the brain must *actively inhibit* the controllosphere drive from dlPFC/ACC and shift to a low-effort, high-uncertainty state. This is implemented via:
1. Prefrontal downregulation (reduced catecholamine drive)
2. DMN engagement (self-referential processing, mental simulation)
3. Vagal activation (HRV increase, parasympathetic tone)
4. ACC-insula decoupling (reduced error monitoring and interoceptive hypervigilance)
