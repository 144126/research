# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What are the proven strategies, tactics, frameworks, and psychological principles that enable a webapp founder to get people to pay immediately upon launch — converting day-one visitors into paying customers without requiring months of free usage, audience building, or freemium traction?

## Purpose & Context
This research serves founders and solo developers launching webapps (primarily SaaS B2B tools, but also covering B2C and marketplace models) who want to avoid the "build first, then pray" failure mode. The central question is: how do you design your product, pricing, distribution, and sales process so that people pay on day one — not after a free trial, not after months of building an audience, not after giving away value for free first?

The stakes are massive: 96.7% of newly launched SaaS sites never monetize [MRRScout 2026, 8,108-site study]. Founders who delay pricing or launch without a payment-integrated product are running experiments that suppress the most informative signal — whether someone will actually pay. This research must deliver an actionable, evidence-backed playbook.

### Audience
- Primary: Indie founders, solo developers, technical co-founders launching their first webapp — builders who can code but struggle with monetization, pricing, and sales
- Secondary: Product managers, startup advisors, and investors evaluating early-stage monetization strategy
- Tone: Precise, data-driven, actionable — no fluff, no theory without evidence
- Complexity: Practitioner-level depth — frameworks, step-by-step playbooks, specific numbers and benchmarks, case studies with real revenue data

### Decision Context
Founders deciding between charging from day one vs. offering free access; choosing pricing models; structuring pre-sell campaigns; designing day-one monetization flows.

### Research Scope
**In Scope:**
1. Pre-selling before building — deposit frameworks, pre-order campaigns, refundable-deposit strategies
2. Day-one pricing strategies — value-based pricing, price skimming, anchoring, Van Westendorp, willingness-to-pay testing
3. Concierge MVP / manual service delivery — charging for the outcome before automating it with software
4. Launch monetization tactics — Product Hunt conversion optimization, waitlist-to-paid conversion, scarcity/urgency mechanics
5. Distribution channels that produce paying customers at launch (not just signups)
6. Psychological levers — scarcity, urgency, social proof, loss aversion, commitment bias, price-quality heuristic
7. Case studies of webapps/startups that achieved day-one revenue
8. Counterarguments and failure modes — when charging early kills the product

**Out of Scope:**
- General SaaS growth/marketing after the first 100 customers
- Enterprise sales cycles longer than 30 days
- Hardware or physical product launches
- Ad-based or data-monetization business models
- Venture-funding strategy

### Timeframe
Primary emphasis: 2023-2026 (post-AI-boom SaaS landscape). Includes foundational sources from 2010-2022 where they established lasting principles.

### Geographic Focus
Global with emphasis on English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia, EU).
