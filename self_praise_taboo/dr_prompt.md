# Deep Research Prompt: Self-Praise Taboo Across Cultures

## Research Question
Why do cultures — particularly East Asian and Judeo-Christian traditions — prohibit self-praise ("do not say you are good with your own mouth"), and what are the psychological, linguistic, and social dimensions of this taboo? How do modern contexts (social media, job markets, personal branding) conflict with or reshape this norm?

## Audience & Purpose
Primary: A 54-year-old academic who wants a rigorous, citation-heavy analysis. Secondary: His 9-year-old who should also be able to follow it — so every complex term gets an inline [imagine: ...] explanation. The report informs a personal essay he is writing on humility and self-promotion in the modern age.

## Scope
In: Self-praise as a social/linguistic act, cultural variation (East Asian vs Western), religious roots (Confucian, Christian, Buddhist), psychological research on humility/modesty, pragmatic strategies for self-praise (humblebrag, self-deprecation), social media self-presentation, job-market/résumé norms, gender differences in self-praise, the "modesty effect" in self-report surveys, Chinese idioms about self-praise (自卖自夸, 王婆卖瓜), Biblical proverbs (Proverbs 27:2), the humblebrag phenomenon.
Out: Corporate branding, political propaganda, celebrity culture generally, advertising ethics, detailed discussion of specific social media platforms' algorithms, non-human animals.

## Dimensions (all 13 required)
1. Technical — Linguistic/pragmatic frameworks for analyzing self-praise as a speech act (face theory, politeness theory, speech act theory)
2. Historical — Origins of the self-praise taboo in ancient texts (Proverbs, Analects, Dao De Jing, Buddhist scriptures)
3. State-of-art — Current academic research on self-praise across cultures (Xie & Tong 2022, Kim & Kim 2025, CMS scale)
4. Quantitative — Cross-cultural survey data on self-enhancement vs modesty, World Values Survey happiness reporting differences, modesty scale validations
5. Stakeholders — Who benefits from the self-praise taboo? Who is harmed? (employers, job seekers, women, minorities, social media influencers)
6. Competing approaches — Self-enhancement vs self-effacement; assertive self-promotion vs humblebragging vs silent competence
7. Criticisms — Is the self-praise taboo oppressive? Does it hold back women and minorities in Westernized workplaces? Is it a tool of social control?
8. Contrarian — Self-praise can be honest and necessary; the taboo may be outdated in a globalized economy; "blowing your own trumpet" is a skill, not a sin
9. Future — Will AI-generated content, personal branding culture, and globalized hiring erode the self-praise taboo? Are younger generations less bound by it?
10. Regulatory — Workplace codes of conduct, HR policies on self-promotion, academic hiring norms, LinkedIn community guidelines on boasting
11. Geographic — East Asia (China, Japan, Korea), Southeast Asia, Middle East, Western Europe, North America, Latin America — how each region negotiates self-praise
12. Practical — Concrete strategies for self-praise without social penalty across cultures; how to navigate the taboo in multicultural teams
13. Public opinion / user sentiment — Reddit, Hacker News, Twitter/X discussions about humblebragging, LinkedIn cringe culture, Quora threads on self-promotion
