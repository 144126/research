# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Research Question
What are the true underlying reasons, causes, and root mechanisms of Solomon's paradox — the psychological phenomenon where people reason more wisely about others' problems than their own?

## Core Components Decomposed
1. **Phenomenon definition**: Self-other asymmetry in wise reasoning (Grossmann & Kross 2014)
2. **Proximal mechanisms**: Self-distancing, psychological distance, reduced emotional arousal
3. **Distal mechanisms**: Neural circuits (mPFC vs TPJ), evolutionary design, cognitive architecture
4. **Moderators**: Age, culture, individual differences, intervention effects
5. **Critiques**: Measurement validity, construct validity, replication status

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- Igor Grossmann (Waterloo) — principal investigator, wisdom-as-skills framework
- Ethan Kross (Michigan) — emotion regulation, self-distancing interventions
- Xu et al. (Nanjing Normal) — Chinese replication, self-transcendence pathway
- Chicago Center for Practical Wisdom — applied wisdom research
- Skeptics/critics — construct validity concerns

## Scope Boundaries
**In scope:** All mechanisms, 1990-2026, empirical research, cross-cultural data
**Out of scope:** Biblical analysis, self-help, AI wisdom, non-human animals

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. Solomon's paradox is a real, replicable phenomenon
2. Self-distancing is the primary corrective mechanism
3. Neural substrates differ for self vs. other reasoning
4. The effect is universal across cultures
5. Interventions can reduce the asymmetry
