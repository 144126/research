# Phase 2: PLAN

## Research Strategy

### Primary Sources (Priority Order)
1. **User-generated demand signals** — Reddit r/chess, r/lichess, Chess.com forums, feature request boards, GitHub issues, app store reviews (BEST signal of unmet needs)
2. **Existing tool analysis** — Product pages, feature lists, pricing pages of all existing chess software (shows what's currently available and what's missing)
3. **Market data** — Industry reports, market research, funding data (sizes the opportunity)
4. **Expert commentary** — Developer blogs, founder interviews, GM tool recommendations, YouTube reviews
5. **Academic** — HCI research on chess learning, spaced repetition studies, AI coaching papers

### Knowledge Dependencies
1. First: Map the full landscape of existing chess tools (what exists)
2. Then: Identify gaps and pain points (what's missing/broken)
3. Then: Validate demand (is there real hunger or just developer imagination?)
4. Then: Size the opportunity (market, conversion, pricing)
5. Then: Design product concepts (features, UX, monetization)
6. Then: Assess feasibility (tech stack, build effort, team size)

### Search Query Strategy

**Batch 1 (Landscape Mapping):**
- chess software market size 2025 2026
- best chess apps 2026 comparison
- chess tool ecosystem landscape
- chess open source tools github

**Batch 2 (Pain Points & Gaps):**
- reddit chess feature wishlist what missing
- chess app complaints most requested feature
- chess training tool problems UX friction
- why chess opening trainers not popular

**Batch 3 (Willingness to Pay):**
- chess.com premium worth it reddit
- chess software pricing comparison
- chess coaching cost per hour 2026
- chess app subscription fatigue

**Batch 4 (Deep Dives):**
- AI chess coach natural language analysis tools 2026
- spaced repetition chess opening trainer comparison
- smart chess board market adoption challenges
- chess learning platform retention churn

**Batch 5 (Contrarian/Verification):**
- why chess startups fail
- chess market saturated
- chess app uninstall reasons
- do chess players actually want to improve

### Triangulation Plan
- Cross-reference user complaints (Reddit) with app store reviews (App Store) with market data (reports)
- Compare claimed pain points against existing tool features (are people complaining about things that already exist?)
- Validate willingness to pay (what people SAY vs what they DO — subscription data, funding rounds)

### Quality Gates
- Gate 1: 3+ independent sources per major claim
- Gate 2: At least 1 contrarian/negative source per opportunity
- Gate 3: Every market size figure has traceable source
- Gate 4: Product concepts reference specific existing tools

### Estimation
- Phase 3 (Retrieve): 10-15 batches, ~30-45 min
- Phase 4 (Triangulate): 5-10 min
- Phase 4.5 (Outline Refine): 5 min
- Phase 5-7 (Synthesize/Critique/Refine): 15-20 min
- Phase 8 (Package): 15-20 min
