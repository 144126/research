# Phase 1: SCOPE Document

## Research Question
What are the mechanisms and explanations for placebo and nocebo effects across all relevant disciplines — including psychology, neurology, philosophy, physics, metaphysics — and what real-world stories/experiences illustrate these phenomena, especially strange/unexpected cases?

## Core Components
1. **Definition & History** - Origins of placebo/nocebo concepts, evolution of understanding
2. **Neurological Mechanisms** - Brain networks, neurotransmitters (dopamine, endorphins, cannabinoids, CCK), neural circuitry, imaging studies
3. **Psychological Mechanisms** - Expectation, classical conditioning, meaning response, suggestibility, personality traits
4. **Philosophical Implications** - Mind-body problem, downward causation, ontological status of mental causation, epistemology of placebo
5. **Physics/Metaphysics Perspectives** - Observer effect parallels, quantum biology, non-local consciousness theories, energy medicine
6. **Real-World Stories** - Documented strange cases, spontaneous healing, nocebo deaths, voodoo death, placebo surgery, unexpected reversals
7. **Clinical & Experimental Evidence** - Meta-analyses, systematic reviews, key experiments (open-hidden paradigm, conditioning studies, imaging)
8. **Ethical & Cultural Dimensions** - Deception ethics, ritual healing across cultures, cross-cultural variations

## Scope Boundaries
**IN:**
- All explanatory frameworks (biomedical, psychological, philosophical, metaphysical)
- Strange/unexpected/counterintuitive cases
- Interdisciplinary connections and syntheses
- Historical development of ideas
- Cross-cultural healing traditions
- Ethical implications of placebo use

**OUT:**
- Specific drug development or clinical trial methodology (except as illustrative)
- Detailed statistical methods of meta-analyses
- Pure pharmacology unrelated to placebo mechanisms
- Alternative medicine efficacy claims without mechanistic discussion

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- Medical researchers and clinicians
- Neuroscientists studying brain-body pathways
- Psychologists of expectation and conditioning
- Philosophers of mind and science
- Metaphysicians and consciousness researchers
- Patients with remarkable experiences
- Ethicists concerned with deception in medicine

## Key Assumptions
1. Placebo and nocebo effects are real, measurable phenomena with multiple mechanisms
2. Different explanatory frameworks may be complementary rather than contradictory
3. Strange cases provide valuable data even if not reproducible in controlled settings
4. Interdisciplinary synthesis can yield insights unavailable within single disciplines
5. Subjective experience is a valid data domain when properly documented

## Success Criteria
- 216+ unique sources across all domains
- At least 3 sources per major claim
- Coverage of neurology, psychology, philosophy, physics/metaphysics, and real stories
- 4-8 major findings (600-2000 words each)
- Executive summary (800-1500 words)
- Complete bibliography with all citations
- Document verification through evidence.jsonl
