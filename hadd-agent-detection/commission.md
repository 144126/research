# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What is the Hyperactive/Hypersensitive Agency Detection Device (HADD) — why does the theory claim it exists, how does it supposedly work, what is the actual neurological and psychological evidence for and against it, what is its complete intellectual history, and does the "real true reason" for its widespread academic acceptance hold up to scrutiny?

## Purpose & Context
This research is commissioned to provide the definitive account of HADD — one of the most influential yet contested concepts in the cognitive science of religion (CSR). The user wants to understand not just the textbook definition, but the "real true reason" behind the theory: its evolutionary logic, its purported neurological mechanism, the psychological experiments meant to test it, the historical chain of ideas that produced it, and the ongoing crisis around its lack of empirical support. This matters because HADD has been called the "Standard Model" of the evolution of religion, yet a growing number of researchers argue it has no empirical basis after nearly 30 years.

### Audience
- Primary: A critical, curious non-specialist interested in the deep truth behind a famous cognitive science concept
- Tone: Precise, evidence-driven, willing to report controversy and uncertainty
- Complexity: Accessible to educated non-specialists but with full citation depth

### Decision Context
- What can we reliably claim about HADD vs. what is speculation dressed as fact?
- Does HADD survive empirical scrutiny or is it a "just-so story"?
- What is the best current science on why humans detect/personify agency?

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. Complete intellectual history of HADD: origin, progenitors, evolution of the concept
2. The error management theory logic underpinning HADD
3. The proposed mechanism: how HADD supposedly works (perceptual, cognitive, neural)
4. Neurological evidence: pSTS, TPJ, agency detection networks in the brain
5. Psychological experimental evidence: all major studies attempting to test HADD
6. The HADD crisis: null results, publication bias, replication failures
7. Alternative/completing theories: predictive processing (PPAD), motivation-based theory, cultural evolution
8. Applications: religion, conspiracy theories, paranormal belief, superstition, anthropomorphism
9. The debunking argument: does HADD undermine the rationality of religious belief?
10. Current state of the field (2024-2026)

### Out of Scope
- Detailed theological responses to HADD-based debunking arguments
- Cross-cultural surveys of specific religious beliefs (only agency detection itself)
- Other CSR concepts not directly related to agency detection (e.g., MCI theory except where it intersects)
- Philosophical arguments about free will triggered by agency detection research

### Timeframe
Foundational works (1944 Heider-Simmel through 1993 Guthrie through 2000-2004 Barrett) through present (2025-2026). Emphasis on 2019-2026 crisis period.

## Required Dimensions of Investigation

Each dimension must be investigated with specific evidence, citations inline, and clear labeling of claim class (vendor-sourced / user-reported / expert-third-party).

### 1. Historical Context & Intellectual Origins
- What is the complete intellectual lineage of HADD? Who were its real progenitors?
- How did Stewart Guthrie's "Faces in the Clouds" (1993) set the stage?
- How did Justin Barrett develop and popularize the term HADD (2000-2004)?
- How did the concept evolve from "Hyperactive Agent Detection Device" to "Hypersensitive Agency Detection Device"?
- What role did Scott Atran, Pascal Boyer, and others play in the "Standard Model"?
- How did HADD become so widely accepted despite early warnings about lack of evidence?

### 2. Technical/Mechanistic: How Does HADD Supposedly Work?
- What is the exact proposed mechanism? (non-inertial movement detection → agent inference → theory of mind attribution)
- The two-step model: HADD detects presence of agent → mentalizing attributes intentions
- Error Management Theory as the foundational logic (Pascal's wager for cognition)
- The "smoke detector principle" and asymmetric error costs
- What specific perceptual cues supposedly trigger HADD? (self-propelled motion, contingent interaction, goal-directed behavior)
- How does ambiguity and threat supposedly amplify HADD activity?
- What is Barrett's own detailed account of the mechanism?

### 3. Neurological Analysis: The Brain Basis of Agency Detection
- What brain regions are reliably involved in agency detection?
- The right posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS): evidence for its role in biological motion, intention attribution, social perception
- The TPJ-pSTS hub for theory of mind and social cognition
- How the pSTS responds to Heider-Simmel-type animations
- The claim that no dedicated "HADD module" has been found (Lisdorf 2007 critique)
- Predictive processing models vs. modular HADD in neuroscience
- Is there a specific neural substrate that could correspond to HADD, or is agency detection distributed across multiple general-purpose systems?

### 4. Psychological Experimental Evidence
- A comprehensive review of every major experimental study testing HADD predictions
- The Heider-Simmel (1944) paradigm and its modern replications
- Maij et al. (2019): The six threat-induction experiments — what was found?
- Szymanek et al. (2024): The preregistered replication attempt — results?
- Andersen et al. (2017-2019): Multiple studies with null results
- The Cambridge study on "evolution of distorted beliefs" that found HADD prime had no detectable effect
- What were the sample sizes, experimental designs, dependent measures?
- Meta-analytic findings: is there any aggregate evidence for HADD?
- The response bias finding: participants actually biased toward *not* detecting agents

### 5. The HADD Crisis: Criticisms and the "No Evidence" Claim
- Kat Ford's 2024 Skeptic article: "HADD Its Day — there's no evidence"
- Will Gervais and Aiyana Willard's independent null results
- Multiple researchers at conferences reporting the same failure
- Publication bias against null results
- The "just-so story" critique from philosophy of science (e.g., Brit J Phil Sci 2012)
- Modularity critique: no evidence for a dedicated HADD module
- The "Mickey Mouse problem" and other conceptual criticisms
- Atkinson (2024) attempt to salvage HADD by distinguishing origin vs. maintenance
- Van Leeuwen & van Elk (2019) comprehensive review

### 6. Alternative Theories Replacing HADD
- Predictive Processing-based Agency Detection (PPAD): agency detection as Bayesian prior updating rather than specialized module
- Motivation-based theory (Willard et al. 2025): mental state reasoning to explain causally opaque phenomena
- Cultural evolutionary accounts: how religion propagates without requiring HADD
- The shrinking domain of religion with the growth of science
- How these alternatives account for the same phenomena without HADD's evidential problems

### 7. Practical Applications: What HADD Has Been Used to Explain
- Religion and supernatural agent belief (the primary application)
- Conspiracy theory belief (Douglas et al. 2016)
- Paranormal belief and superstition
- Anthropomorphism and seeing faces in clouds
- Political polarization and outgroup demonization
- Existential threat → conspiracy belief mediation pathway (2025 studies)
- How well do these applications hold up given the HADD evidence crisis?

### 8. Contrarian & Heterodox Perspectives
- Could HADD still be correct despite null experimental results? (measurement problems, wrong operationalization)
- The defense by Atkinson (2024): two versions of HADD, one salvageable
- Barrett's rebuttals and modifications over time
- Philosophical defenses: does HADD need to be a "module"?
- The theist perspective: HADD as God's design rather than evolutionary byproduct
- Could HADD be real but experimentally elusive (too automatic, not accessible to self-report)?

### 9. The Debunking Debate
- Does HADD, if true, constitute an evolutionary debunking of religious belief?
- The "HADD Produces Many False Positives" argument
- Theistic responses: HADD could be the mechanism God uses to produce belief
- How the evidence crisis affects the debunking argument
- Reidian religious epistemology vs. HADD-based debunking

### 10. Current State of the Field (2025-2026)
- Where does HADD stand in the cognitive science of religion today?
- Is the "Standard Model" collapsing or evolving?
- Recent preregistered studies and their findings
- The shift toward predictive processing and cultural evolution frameworks
- What major figures currently say about HADD
- Open questions for future research
