**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5664556/
**Authors:** Leyla Isik, Kami Koldewyn, David Beeler, Nancy Kanwisher (MIT, Bangor University)
**Journal:** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS), 2017, 114(43):E9145–E9152
**PMCID:** PMC5664556 | **PMID:** 29073111
**Reviewers:** Alex Martin (NIMH), Kevin Pelphrey (George Washington University)

## Core Finding

A specific subregion of the **right posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS)** responds **strongly and selectively** when viewing social interactions between two agents. This region — termed the **social interaction functional ROI (SI-fROI)** — is dissociable from nearby regions implicated in theory of mind (TPJ), face processing (pSTS-face), and motion processing (MT).

---

## Key Experimental Design

### Five Neural Signatures Tested

1. **Responds more to interacting agents than independent agents**
2. **Works even with minimalist stimuli** (point-light displays, geometric shapes)
3. **Generalizes across stimulus formats and tasks**
4. **Unconfounded from animacy or individual goals**
5. **Contains information about the *nature* of interactions** (helping vs. hindering)

### Experiment 1: Point-Light Walker Dyads
- 14 subjects viewed point-light displays of two people interacting vs. two people acting independently
- Whole-brain group analysis: **right pSTS peak activation [54, −43, 18] (MNI)**
- 12/14 subjects showed significant SI-fROI; all 14 showed greater response to interacting vs. independent in held-out data
- Response ~2x stronger for interacting vs. independent

### Experiment 2: Shape Animations (Heider-Simmel style)
- Four conditions: **help, hinder, physical interaction (billiard balls), animate (single goal-driven shape)**
- SI-fROI responded more to social interactions (help+hinder) than physical interactions (P = 1.3×10⁻⁴)
- **SI-fROI showed no difference** between animate (single agent) vs. physical interaction (P = 0.44) — proving animacy/goals alone do not drive it
- A separate STS region (anterior to SI-fROI) responded to animacy/goal-directed actions

### Helping vs. Hindering Decoding (MVPA)
- Linear SVM classifier could decode help vs. hinder from SI-fROI (P = 1.2×10⁻⁴)
- Also decodable in TPJ (P = 5.0×10⁻⁴) and pSTS-face (P = 0.0086)
- **Not decodable in MT** (P = 0.39) — ruling out low-level visual motion confound

## Spatial Organization of pSTS Subregions

| Region | Location relative to SI-fROI | Function |
|--------|------------------------------|----------|
| **SI-fROI (social interaction)** | Anterior to TPJ, superior to pSTS-face | Perceiving third-party social interactions |
| **TPJ** | Posterior/superior to SI-fROI | Theory of mind (inferring others' thoughts) |
| **pSTS-face region** | Inferior to SI-fROI | Dynamic face/voice processing |
| **Animate/goal region** | Anterior to SI-fROI | Agency/goal-directed actions of individuals |

- SI-fROI overlap with TPJ: ~8% of SI-fROI, ~2% of TPJ
- SI-fROI overlap with pSTS-face: ~1% of SI-fROI, ~1% of pSTS-face
- **Functionally distinct but partially overlapping sensitivities**

## Relevance to HADD (Hyperactive Agency Detection Device)

### Direct Connections

1. **pSTS as a hub for agency detection:** The pSTS is the primary cortical region processing biological motion, goal-directed actions, communicative intent, and social interactions — all components of agency detection
2. **Selectivity for social interactions over individual animacy:** Crucially, the SI-fROI responds to *interactions between agents*, not just individual agents. This suggests the neural substrate for HADD may be more specifically tuned to relational/social agency (agent-agent) than to isolated agency (agent-object)
3. **Valence discrimination (help/hinder):** The pSTS encodes whether an interaction is positive or negative — relevant to HADD's proposed role in threat detection (Barrett's "better safe than sorry" logic)
4. **Automatic/pre-attentive processing:** Activity was observed during passive viewing (no task), supporting HADD's proposed automaticity

### Limitations for HADD Theory

1. **Not a "module" in the strict sense:** The authors describe the SI-fROI as "a peak in the landscape of partially overlapping sensitivities" — not a discrete cortical module with sharp boundaries
2. **No direct evidence for hyperactivity/over-detection:** The study shows selectivity for *genuine* social interactions, not false-positive detection of agency in random stimuli
3. **Right-hemisphere lateralized:** Primarily right pSTS, whereas HADD theory might predict bilateral or more distributed processing
4. **No threat condition tested:** No manipulation of threat/safety to test HADD's prediction of heightened detection under threat

### Independent Replication & Extension

- **Walbrin et al. (2017):** Independent study with different stimuli and subjects found highly similar results, including decoding cooperative vs. competitive interactions from the same region
- **Landsiedel et al. (2022, NeuroImage):** Confirmed bilateral SI-pSTS plays a central role in *dynamic* social interaction perception but is much less involved with static cues; EBA and pSTS work together during dynamic interaction perception
- **Deen et al. (2015, Cerebral Cortex):** Found STS organized along a posterior-to-anterior axis with both domain-specific and overlapping response regions

### Macaque Comparison (Sliwa & Freiwald, 2017)
- Macaques have frontal/parietal regions selectively responsive to social interactions — **not homologous** to human pSTS
- Macaque lateral temporal lobe responds to social interactions but also to object interactions — unlike humans
- Suggests **evolutionary specialization** for social interaction perception in humans

## Neural Regions Processing Agency Detection (Summary)

| Brain Region | Role in Agency/Social Perception |
|-------------|----------------------------------|
| **pSTS (SI-fROI)** | Social interaction perception (help/hinder, cooperative/competitive); dynamic social scene understanding |
| **TPJ** | Theory of mind, mental state attribution, belief reasoning |
| **pSTS-face region** | Dynamic face/voice processing, biological motion |
| **Anterior STS** | Animacy and goal-directed action perception |
| **MT** | Low-level visual motion (not selective for social content) |
| **mPFC** | Mentalizing, social evaluation |
| **Precuneus** | Third-person perspective, social scene processing |
| **EBA (extrastriate body area)** | Body form processing, collaborates with pSTS for dynamic body-based interactions |

## Key Quotes

> "We observe a strong univariate response in the posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) to stimuli depicting social interactions between two agents, compared with pairs of agents not interacting with each other, physical interactions between inanimate objects, and individual animate agents pursuing goals."

> "This sensitivity to social interactions is not easily explainable in terms of low-level visual features, attention, or the animacy, actions, or goals of individual agents."

> "The sensitivity to social interactions reported here may be better considered not as a discrete module, but rather as a peak in the landscape of partially overlapping sensitivities to multiple dimensions of social information in the STS."

> "The currently available evidence suggests the existence of at least four areas of dissociable responses in this general region: the TPJ, specialized for inferring the thoughts of others; the pSTS-face region; another STS region responsive to the agency and/or goal-directed actions of individual actors; and the selective response to social interactions reported here."

## Confidence Assessment

- **Credibility Score: 95/100**
- Rigorous fMRI methodology with group-constrained subject-specific analysis
- Cross-validation with held-out data
- Independent replication (Walbrin et al., 2017)
- Published in PNAS, reviewed by leading social neuroscientists
- Well-powered controls for low-level features, attention, animacy, goals
- Limitation: n=14; fMRI-only (no causal evidence); no developmental data
