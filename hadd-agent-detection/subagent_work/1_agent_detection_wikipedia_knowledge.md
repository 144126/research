# Subagent Knowledge File: HADD — Hyperactive/Hypersensitive Agency Detection Device

## 1. Core Definition (from Wikipedia)

**Agent detection** is "the inclination for animals, including humans, to presume the purposeful intervention of a sentient or intelligent agent in situations that may or may not involve one."

Linked to the evolutionary origins of religion.

---

## 2. Key Claims from Source

### 2.1 Evolutionary Origins

- Humans evolved agent detection as a survival strategy
- In uncertain situations, assuming presence of an agent (enemy/predator) has survival value
- Classic example: indentation in ground that might be a lion's footprint → better to assume lion present
- **Cost asymmetry**: high cost of failing to detect agents (miss) vs low cost of wrongly detecting them (false positive)

### 2.2 HADD (Hyperactive Agent Detection Device)

- Term attributed to Kurt Gray and Daniel Wegner (2010) in the Wikipedia article
- Described as: "a cognitive module that readily ascribes events in the environment to the behavior of agents"
- Wikipedia cites Gray & Wegner, *Personality and Social Psychology Review*, 14(1), 9-10, 2010
- **BUT** external research shows the term was *coined by Justin Barrett* in the late 1990s / early 2000s
- Atkinson (2023) clarifies: "it is Barrett who spearheaded it and defended it, thusly galvanising his name to the concept historically"
- Barrett originally discussed it in lectures before a conference in Finland; refined in his 2004 book *Why Would Anyone Believe in God?*

### 2.3 Signal Detection Theory Framework

| | Response Present | Response Absent |
|---|---|---|
| **Stimuli Present** | Hit | Miss (worst outcome) |
| **Stimuli Absent** | False Positive (less costly) | Correct Rejection |

- False positives enabled survival and higher fitness
- Missing a false positive (i.e., a real agent) can result in injury or death

### 2.4 Two-Step Process (Kühnen et al., 2021)

1. **Step 1**: Action tendency recognized by hypersensitive agency detection (shared with many animals)
2. **Step 2**: Attribution of the agent — understanding beliefs, desires, intentions (mentalization / theory of mind — particularly human)
- Humans project theory of mind to better understand agents

### 2.5 Role in Religion

- Belief in gods = evolutionary by-product of agent detection
- **Spandrel** (Pascal Boyer, *Religion Explained*, 2001): non-adaptive trait formed as side effect of adaptive trait
- The psychological trait: "if you hear a twig snap in the forest, some sentient force is probably behind it"
- Even if snapping was caused by wind, modern humans still inclined to attribute it to a sentient agent; "they call this person a god"
- Religion may have played role in **group cohesion** (Kühnen et al.)
- **Social brain hypothesis** (Dunbar & Shultz, 2007): larger brains → language → shared knowledge → increased fitness
- Sacred rules/roles resolved **free-rider problem** (Haidt, *The Righteous Mind*, 2012, pp. 252-257)

### 2.6 Gray & Wegner's Qualification

- Agent detection is a "foundation for human belief in God"
- BUT "simple over attribution of agency cannot entirely account for the belief in God"
- Theory of mind + "existential theory of mind" also required to "give us the basic cognitive capacity to conceive of God"

### 2.7 Criticism — "Time-consuming steps, fast escapes"

- Evolutionary biologists (Stevens, 2013; Hammerstein & Stevens, 2012) argue:
  - Thinking about *why* a stimulus is present takes time; simple reaction is faster
  - Simple reflexes (running from shape of footprints or pair of eyes) would be selected instead
  - Conclusion: **"no specialized brain mechanisms for agent detection"**

## 3. Verification and Expansion from External Searches

### 3.1 Origins of the Term "HADD"

- **Justin Barrett** is the actual originator of the term, not Gray & Wegner (who used it in passing)
- Barrett, a cognitive scientist, developed HADD in the late 1990s/early 2000s
- Key publications:
  - "Exploring the Natural Foundations of Religion," *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 2000, vol.4 pp 29-34
  - *Why Would Anyone Believe in God?* (AltaMira, 2004)
- Atkinson (2023) paper "Who put the 'H' in the HADD?" traces origin:
  - Correspondence with McCauley, Barrett, and Lawson
  - Barrett had been talking about agency detection in lectures well before Finland conference
  - Not one single author, but Barrett "spearheaded it and defended it, thusly galvanising his name to the concept historically"
  - Early discussions meant to be cognitive specification of Stewart Guthrie's "anthropomorphism" concept

### 3.2 Neural Basis Claims (Novella, 2010)

- Steven Novella (neurologist): "our brains are hardwired to distinguish things in our environment that are alive from those that are not alive"
- Visual system separates information about actions vs objects
- Object stream divided into: brain regions for inanimate objects vs regions for living/animate things
- HADD works partly by detecting **non-inertial movement** — things moving of their own volition
- HADD more likely triggered when stimulus is **ambiguous** → default assumption = agent
- HADD more active in situations where we have **less control**
- But Novella: "So far this hypothesis has not been significantly researched"

### 3.3 Lisdorf (2007) — "What's HIDD'n in the HADD?"

- Proposed **HIDD** (Hyperactive Intentionality Detection Device) instead of HADD
- Argued: "the HADD does not work that way" when considering neural basis
- Suggested HADD explanation may be a "self-conjuring trick"
- Called for more parsimonious explanation
- Suggested intentionality detection bias rather than agent detection bias

### 3.4 Maij et al. (2019) — Boundary Conditions

- 6 threat-inducing experiments (N=233)
- Tests: Biological Motion Detection Task, Auditory Agent Detection Task, Geometrical Figures Task
- Findings: "threat manipulations that worked as intended did not increase agent detection compared to a control condition"
- Participants showed general response bias toward perceiving intentionality (Geometrical Figures Task)
- "These results indicate that the frequently postulated relationship between threat and agency detection is not as straightforward as often assumed"
- Supports Lisdorf's suggestion: intentionality detection bias > agent detection bias

### 3.5 Van Leeuwen, Szymanek, & Nenadalová (2024) — Preregistered VR Study

- 199 participants explored agentless foggy virtual forest
- Instructed to press button when perceiving a being
- Compared dangerous vs safe environments
- **Key finding**: "Contrary to HADD expectations, no elevated false agent detections occurred under threat"
- Instead: detection aligned more with prior expectations and sensory reliability
- "Challenging evolutionary hyperactivity accounts and favoring adaptive, context-sensitive models"
- Published in *Psychology of Religion and Spirituality* (APA journal)
- Support for **predictive processing model** over HADD

### 3.6 Kat Ford (2024, The Skeptic) — "No Evidence"

- PhD from Centre for Culture and Evolution, Brunel University
- Tried to investigate link between HADD and religious belief during PhD: "found… nothing"
- Will Gervais and Aiyana Willard also tried: "found no evidence that HADD even existed"
- Willard approached by "multiple other researchers who similarly spent time conducting research based on the idea that the HADD was the key to understanding religious cognition"
- "No empirical evidence that the hyperactive agency detection device exists"
- Concept has been around since 1990s but remains empirically unsubstantiated

### 3.7 Predictive Processing Alternative

- Multiple researchers (van Elk, Andersen, Van Leeuwen, etc.) argue agency detection best explained by:
  - **Learned priors** and cultural influences
  - **Predictive processing**: perception = best guess based on prior expectations + sensory input
  - Not a dedicated hyperactive module
- Andersen (2019) VR study: over-attribution occurred more for intentional-like actions but emphasized context-dependent Bayesian updating
- Van Leeuwen & van Elk (2019): agency detection biases better explained by learned priors than dedicated HADD module
- Schjoedt et al. (2023): neuroimaging data shows variability across populations

### 3.8 Links to Paranormal/Conspiracy

- HADD linked to conspiracy theorizing (hidden agents behind events)
- Correlates with paranoia symptoms — specific social-cognitive pathway
- Paranoid ideation: neutral events interpreted as deliberate threats
- Experimental evidence: individuals with heightened agency attribution biases endorse more conspiracy ideas
- Effect stronger among those scoring high on schizotypy measures
- BUT: correlational, not necessarily causal

## 4. Synthesis of Expert Consensus (Current State)

### What is supported:
- Humans (and many animals) have a **bias toward agency attribution** — this is well-established
- **Error Management Theory** (Haselton & Buss, 1999): false negatives cost more than false positives in ancestral environments
- Agency detection involves distributed brain systems (fusiform face area, temporo-parietal junction, etc.) — not one "module"

### What is contested / unsupported:
- **No dedicated "hyperactive module"** has been identified in the brain
- **No consistent empirical evidence** that threat increases false agent detection (Maij 2019, Szymanek 2024)
- **No evidence** that HADD specifically predicts religious belief (Ford PhD, Gervais & Willard)
- The "HADD" concept is **theoretically useful but empirically underdetermined**

### Leading alternative framework:
- **Predictive processing** (PP) model: agency detection = Bayesian inference combining prior expectations with sensory input
- PP explains the same phenomena without postulating a special-purpose module
- Agency detection is **domain-general** pattern recognition, not hyperactive-specific

### Credibility Considerations:
- Wikipedia article is a reasonable summary but dated in some respects
- Article correctly identifies criticism from evolutionary biology
- Article attributes HADD to Gray & Wegner rather than Barrett (minor inaccuracy)
- Article does not cover the 2019-2025 empirical challenges to HADD
- The field is currently shifting from HADD toward predictive processing approaches
