**Author:** Steven Novella, MD (neurologist, Yale; host of Skeptics' Guide to the Universe)
**Published:** 2010-03-22
**Category:** Neuroscience / Skepticism

## 1. Core Claims About HADD Mechanism

### 1.1 Term Origin
- HADD = "hyperactive (or hypersensitive) agency detection device" — coined by **Justin Barrett** (cognitive scientist, University of Oxford; later Fuller Seminary).
- Novella presents HADD as "an intrinsic part of human nature" and "core knowledge base of the skeptic."

### 1.2 Non-Inertial Movement as Trigger
- **Central mechanistic claim:** HADD detects **non-inertial movement** — motion that appears self-propelled, not explainable as passive response to natural forces.
- Quote: *"According to Barrett, HADD works in part by detecting any movement that is non-inertial – something which seems to be moving of its own volition. We then assume it is acting with agency and react accordingly."*
- Novella extends this: HADD scales from simple motion detection to complex pattern detection — unrelated events, details defying explanation, disproportionate consequences — all attributed to hidden agents.

### 1.3 Evolutionary Logic
- Classic error-management / "better safe than sorry" framing:
- *"It is better to assume the rustling in the bushes was not the wind but a hungry tiger. So we are descended from hominids who were more paranoid and had hyperactive agency detection, because they were less likely to be eaten by predators."*
- False positives (seeing agency where none exists) are evolutionarily cheap; false negatives (missing a real agent) are catastrophically expensive.

### 1.4 Default Assumption = Agency
- HADD is the **default** — agents are assumed until proven otherwise.
- Triggered more strongly by: **ambiguous stimuli** and **situations of low control**.
- Quote: *"Studies have also demonstrated that HADD is more likely to be triggered when a stimulus is ambiguous – therefore it tends to be our default assumption – an object is an agent until we are sure it's just an object. Also, in situations where we have less control our HADD becomes more active still."*

## 2. Neurological / Visual System Basis

### 2.1 Visual Stream Architecture
- Novella references the **dual-stream visual system**: one stream for **actions**, another for **objects**.
- Within the object stream: separate brain regions for **inanimate objects** vs. **living/animate objects**.
- Quote: *"So on a fundamental level our brains treat agents different than objects – from the moment we see them."*

### 2.2 Agent vs. Object Ontology (via Bruce Hood)
- Novella cites **Bruce Hood** (author of *Supersense*): humans imbue agents with an **essence** — a unique living force — while objects are interchangeable.
- Infants already distinguish agents from objects.
- This explains emotional reactions to cartoons (non-living but perceived as agents) and children's attachment to stuffed toys as pseudo-agents.

### 2.3 Verification from Neuroscience Literature (post-2010)
- **Frontiers in Human Neuroscience (2014):** Agency is the **default cause of motion** in infancy; mechanical explanations must override this default via structure-mapping inferences in the premotor system. Agency remains the default into adulthood unless actively overridden.
- **Journal of Neuroscience (2023; fMRI MVPA study):** Right lateral occipitotemporal cortex (LOTC) and posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) show **higher sensitivity to animacy and agency cues**; left dorsal premotor cortex (PMd) and superior parietal lobules are more sensitive to physics-driven (inanimate) motion. A shared neural representation exists in frontoparietal and posterior temporal regions, but the LOTC/pSTS is specifically tuned for agentive events.

## 3. HADD and Conspiracy Theories

- Novella explicitly links HADD overactivation to conspiracy thinking:
- Quote: *"When HADD is triggered we tend to see a hidden agent working behind the scenes, making events unfold the way they do, and perhaps even deliberately hiding its own tracks."*
- Quote: *"For some people the perception of hidden agency becomes overwhelming, dominating all other thought processes. We know these people as conspiracy theorists. But there is a little conspiracy theorist inside each of us."*
- **Verified by:** Douglas, Sutton, Callan, Dawtry & Harvey (2016) — *"Someone is pulling the strings: Hypersensitive agency detection and belief in conspiracy theories"* (Thinking and Reasoning, 22, 57-77). Directly confirms a link between HADD and conspiracy belief.

## 4. HADD and Religion

- Barrett and others speculated HADD is important to the **development of religion**:
- Quote: *"God is the ultimate invisible agent."*
- Quote: *"Seeing natural or random events as the will of an agent is HADD."*
- Novella notes the hypothesis "has not been significantly researched" (as of 2010).
- **Post-2010 research status:**
  - Templeton Foundation funded "Detecting God's Action" project (2012-2014) specifically to test HADD's role in supernatural agent belief.
  - Maij et al. (2017, *Religion, Brain & Behavior*): Six threat-induction experiments (N=233) using biological motion detection, auditory agent detection, and VR — **found no increase** in false agent detection under threat; participants showed a response bias toward **assuming agents were absent**. HADD measures were **unrelated** to supernatural beliefs.
  - Valdesolo & Graham (2014, *Psychological Science*): Awe increases supernatural belief and intentional-pattern perception via **reduced tolerance for uncertainty** — not directly threat, but consistent with HADD's ambiguity/low-control prediction.
  - **VR study (ResearchGate, 2018):** 90%+ of participants falsely detected agents in VR; but false agency detection was **not related** to supernatural belief (unexpectedly related only to Feng Shui belief).
  - **Mixed evidence:** Barrett & Lanman (2008) argue HADD encourages/strengthens pre-existing supernatural beliefs rather than directly causing them. HADD may be necessary but insufficient; mentalizing (theory of mind) is also required.

## 5. HADD and Superstition

- Quote: *"HADD also leads to superstition – thinking that there is a cause and effect between unconnected events. The underlying assumption of superstitions is that things happen for a reason (a vague ill-defined reason, but a sense that there is a hidden agency at work)."*
- Novella contrasts this with accepting randomness: *"I think it is liberating to understand that rather (as was most elegantly stated on a bumper sticker) 'shit happens.'"*

---

## 6. Skepticism as Corrective Filter

- Novella frames skepticism as a **deliberate filter on HADD**:
- Recognition that brains are "not perfect perceivers and processors."
- Science and skepticism = methods for correcting biases.
- Skeptics ask: "Is it really true?" — patterns must be validated against reality.

## 9. Conflicting / Contradictory Evidence

- **Maij et al. (2017)** — six experiments, N=233, found **no effect of threat on agent detection**. Participants showed bias toward **agent absence**, not presence. This directly challenges the core HADD prediction and Novella's claim that ambiguous/threatening situations increase false agency detection.
- **VR agency detection studies** — false detection rates are high (90%+) but **uncorrelated with supernatural belief**, undermining the HADD→religion link.
- **Barrett himself** (in later writings) downgraded HADD from a direct cause to a contributing/enabling factor, emphasizing mentalizing (theory of mind) as a necessary second step.
- The academic literature is increasingly cautious: boundary conditions exist, and the HADD's role in religion may be weaker than initially theorized.
