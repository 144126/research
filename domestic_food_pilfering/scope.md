# Research Scope: Domestic Food Pilfering

## Research Question
Why do people compulsively take food from the fridge (domestic food pilfering) when they know they shouldn't, and what fundamental first-principles knowledge would eliminate the desire at its root?

## Question Type
Exploratory + Decision-support — understanding a personally-felt behavior through first-principles to enable transformation, not management.

## Audience
- Primary: Intellectually-curious adult with history of failed willpower approaches
- Secondary: Child (9+ years) — every hard concept needs [imagine: ...] ELI5
- Tone: Academic depth, rigorous, grounded in concrete mechanisms

## Constraints
- No tips/tricks/hacks — only fundamental understanding
- No diet plans or nutritional recommendations
- Must distinguish desire-reduction from suppression
- Timeframe: 1990-2026 (primary), 2020-2026 (behavioral interventions)
- Geographic: Primarily US/Western, cross-cultural where available

## 12 Dimensions of Investigation

| # | Dimension | Focus |
|---|-----------|-------|
| 1 | Technical/Mechanistic | Neurobiology of wanting vs liking, incentive salience, dopamine circuits, Pavlovian-instrumental transfer |
| 2 | Historical | Evolution from Freud/Hull/Berridge to modern food addiction concept |
| 3 | State-of-Art | Food noise 2024-2026, GLP-1 effects, UPF lawsuits, habit interventions |
| 4 | Quantitative | Prevalence rates, effect sizes, YFAS data, weight regain stats |
| 5 | Stakeholders | Berridge, Gearhardt, Brewer, Wood, Clear; food industry, FDA, NIH |
| 6 | Competing Approaches | Restriction vs permission, pharmacological vs behavioral, top-down vs bottom-up |
| 7 | Criticisms | UPF model criticism, intuitive eating concerns, willpower-as-muscle replication failures |
| 8 | Contrarian | Willpower as wrong tool, food morality trap, restriction as driver, white bear problem |
| 9 | Future | DSM-6 classification, GLP-1 dominance, food noise construct evolution |
| 10 | Regulatory/Legal | San Francisco UPF lawsuit, labeling, taxation, industry lobbying |
| 11 | Geographic | US vs Europe vs developing nations, cultural variation in restriction |
| 12 | Applications | Brewer's curiosity approach, Clear's environmental design, habit reversal, case studies |

## Source Target
- **Hard floor: 1440+ sources** (broad behavioral topic with many sub-dimensions)
- Target distribution: 20%+ academic, 15%+ industry, 10%+ open-source, 5%+ news, 5%+ government
- At least 4 distinct source types
- Mix of foundational (pre-2020) and recent (2024-2026)
- Both proponents AND critics of food addiction model
- Geographic diversity: US, Europe, Brazil, Australia

## Scope Boundaries
- **In**: food noise, wanting/liking, forbidden fruit effect, UPF addiction, habit loops, environmental design, mindful/curiosity-based approaches
- **Out**: diet plans, weight loss programs, clinical ED treatment protocols, GLP-1 pharmacology details, motivational coaching, fad diets
