# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
Why does Amazon UK reject a vitamin E supplement listing with "labeling discrepancies" when the packaging states "Made in the UK, marketed for Nigeria," and what exact label edits, documentation, and resubmission strategy will get the ASIN approved and live on Amazon.co.uk?

## Purpose & Context
An Amazon UK seller has been trying for weeks to list a vitamin E supplement (Health & Beauty category) on Amazon.co.uk. The product's physical packaging states "Made in the UK, marketed for Nigeria" alongside the full product description, ingredients, usage instructions, and warnings. Amazon's compliance system repeatedly rejects the listing citing "labeling discrepancies" with no further specific explanation. The seller has exhausted standard appeals and needs a precise, actionable solution.

This research will inform a specific listing rescue operation. The reader (the seller) needs surgical precision — not general guidance. Every finding must translate to a concrete action: what to change on the label, what documents to submit, how to phrase the appeal, which Amazon team to contact, and in what order.

The stakes are financial: inventory sitting unsold, storage costs accruing, and lost revenue every week the product is not live. If the answer is "this label configuration cannot be approved," the seller needs to know definitively so they can relabel. If the answer is "change X, Y, Z and resubmit," every detail must be specific.

### Audience
- **Primary:** An Amazon UK third-party seller with a vitamin E supplement product. Experienced with Seller Central but not a regulatory expert. Needs step-by-step instructions they can execute themselves.
- **Secondary:** Amazon compliance consultants/freelancers who might be hired to fix this.
- **Tone:** Precise, directive, actionable. No theory without practice. Every section must end with "Therefore, do X."
- **Complexity:** Practical/operational level. Explain regulatory requirements only insofar as they dictate specific label changes.
- **Child-reader adaptivity:** Not needed for this practical audience.

### Decision Context
- **What decision will this research inform?** Whether to modify the existing label artwork and resubmit under the same ASIN, create a new ASIN with redesigned packaging, or abandon the UK channel and sell elsewhere.
- **What are the stakes?** The seller's capital is tied up in inventory. Every week without a listing is a week of zero revenue plus potential storage fees.
- **What would change if the answer is X vs Y?** If a simple label edit works (e.g., removing "marketed for Nigeria"), the fix is cheap and fast. If Amazon requires a fundamentally different label design (new ASIN, new barcode), the cost and timeline are significant.

### In Scope
1. The specific incompatibility between "Made in the UK, marketed for Nigeria" and Amazon UK's supplement listing requirements — exactly why this phrase triggers rejection
