# Deep Research Prompt: Fundamental Nature of Reality

## Research Question
What is the fundamental nature of reality? Produce a panoramic, structured map of everything we know and don't know — spanning physics, metaphysics, information theory, emergence/complexity, and consciousness — with emphasis on unsolved problems, controversies, and contrarian positions. This is a "map of the territory" report.

## Audience & Purpose
PhD-adjacent reader who wants a comprehensive structured overview. Purpose: to understand the complete intellectual landscape of fundamental reality research — what is settled, what is debated, what is speculative, and where the biggest open questions lie.

## Scope
In: Quantum Gravity (string theory, LQG, causal sets, asymptotically safe gravity), Grand Unified Theories, Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Bohmian, QBism, objective collapse), Information-theoretic approaches (digital physics, pancomputationalism, Wheeler's "it from bit", Wolfram Physics, constructor theory), Emergence & complexity (self-organization, fractal patterns, pattern field theory, complex adaptive systems, phase transitions), Consciousness & reality (hard problem, IIT, panpsychism, Russellian monism, global workspace theory, integrated world model theory, the combination problem), Metaphysics & ontology (fundamentality, grounding, mereology, structural realism, ontic structural realism, neutral monism, idealism vs materialism vs panpsychism), Arrow of time, problem of time in quantum gravity, cosmological natural selection, fine-tuning & anthropic reasoning, Experimental approaches to quantum gravity (interferometry, phenomenology, tabletop experiments, astrophysical tests).

Out: Theology (unless directly relevant), pure engineering, specific mathematical derivations, detailed biographical history of physicists, political dimensions of physics funding.

Timeframe: Foundational works (Ancient Greece, 19th-20th century) + state-of-art (2020-2026) with emphasis on current active debates.

Geography: Global (Western, Asian, and Global South research traditions where relevant).

## Dimensions (all 13 required)
1. Technical, 2. Historical, 3. State-of-art, 4. Quantitative, 5. Stakeholders, 6. Competing approaches, 7. Criticisms, 8. Contrarian, 9. Future, 10. Regulatory, 11. Geographic, 12. Practical, 13. Public opinion

## Sources
Types: academic papers (arXiv, journals), authoritative textbooks, encyclopedia entries (Stanford Encyclopedia, Internet Encyclopedia, Grokipedia), expert interviews, survey data, community discussions, YouTube lectures from recognized experts, FQXi essays, conference proceedings.
Min 270 sources. At least 5 types.

## Output
Executive summary (1200-1500 words), hundreds of findings with [N] citations, synthesis & insights, limitations & caveats, recommendations, full bibliography, methodology appendix.

## Quality
[N] on every factual claim. Evidence classes labeled. "Weakest Evidence" section. 3+ independent sources per major claim.
