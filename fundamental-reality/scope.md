# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Research Question
What is the fundamental nature of reality? A panoramic, structured map of everything we know and don't know — spanning physics, metaphysics, information theory, emergence/complexity, and consciousness.

## Core Components
1. Quantum Gravity & Unification — string theory, LQG, causal sets, asymptotically safe gravity, GUTs
2. Interpretations of Quantum Mechanics — Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Bohmian, QBism, objective collapse
3. Information-Theoretic Approaches — digital physics, pancomputationalism, Wheeler's "it from bit", Wolfram Physics, constructor theory
4. Emergence & Complexity — self-organization, fractal patterns, pattern field theory, complex adaptive systems, phase transitions
5. Consciousness & Reality — hard problem, IIT, panpsychism, Russellian monism, GWT, integrated world model theory, combination problem
6. Metaphysics & Ontology — fundamentality, grounding, mereology, structural realism, ontic structural realism, neutral monism, idealism vs materialism vs panpsychism
7. Time — arrow of time, problem of time in QG, cosmological natural selection, fine-tuning & anthropic reasoning
8. Experimental Approaches — quantum gravity phenomenology, interferometry, tabletop experiments, astrophysical tests

## Scope Boundaries
- IN: all major research programs in fundamental physics, quantum foundations, philosophy of mind, metaphysics
- OUT: theology (except where directly relevant), pure engineering, specific mathematical derivations, biographical history
- Timeframe: Ancient Greece to 2026, emphasis on active debates 2020-2026

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources across 5+ types (academic, encyclopedia, surveys, community discussions, YouTube lectures)
- All 13 dimensions addressed (technical, historical, state-of-art, quantitative, stakeholders, competing approaches, criticisms, contrarian, future, regulatory, geographic, practical, public opinion)
- Executive summary 1200-1500 words
- Every factual claim cited [N]
- Evidence classes labeled

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. There is genuine disagreement about fundamental ontology among qualified researchers
2. Experimental tests are possible in principle for some QM interpretations
3. The hard problem of consciousness remains unsolved
4. Information-theoretic approaches to physics are gaining traction
5. No single paradigm commands majority consensus
