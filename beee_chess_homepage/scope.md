# Scope: Beee Chess Homepage Research

## Research Question
Should Beee Chess — a chess training SPA playing vs Stockfish with AI coaching, token-based monetization via Paystack — have a separate homepage/landing page? If so, what content, copy, and design should it use?

## In Scope
1. Product-led growth (PLG) vs traditional homepage — when each is appropriate for utility apps
2. SEO impact of having no homepage vs a minimal one
3. Conversion rate comparison: app-as-homepage vs dedicated landing page for tool-like SaaS
4. Minimal homepage content patterns for single-product tools (hero, value prop, CTAs, social proof)
5. Copywriting patterns for chess/education/gaming tools
6. Design patterns for minimal homepages in 2026 (color, layout, typography, visual weight)
7. Chess-related website examples and their conversion strategies
8. The "should I bother" question — when a homepage is truly unnecessary (low traffic, referral-only, API-first)

## Out of Scope
1. Full website redesign or rebranding (this is about adding 1 page)
2. Marketing strategy beyond first-visitor conversion
3. Analytics/measurement setup
4. Blog/content marketing strategy
5. Social media or growth channels
6. Pricing page (uses in-app tokens, no separate pricing tier)
7. Competitive analysis of other chess apps' full marketing funnels

## Success Criteria
- Clear recommendation: build a homepage or don't
- If yes: specific content hierarchy, copy examples, design mockup in words
- If no: conditions under which a homepage would become worthwhile
- Confidence: based on 270+ sources across PLG, conversion, design, and chess domains

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. A homepage improves organic discovery (SEO) for a single-page app
2. The absence of a homepage hurts conversion from organic/direct traffic
3. PLG companies with app-as-homepage succeed under specific conditions that may or may not apply here
4. Minimal homepages outperform elaborate ones for tools/utilities
5. Chess players respond to specific value props (improvement, analysis, coaching) over generic ones
