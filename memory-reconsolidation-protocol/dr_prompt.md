# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What is the complete, step-by-step practical protocol for applying memory reconsolidation to change one's own subconscious beliefs — synthesizing the RECON protocol (Courtney Armstrong), the Therapeutic Reconsolidation Process / TRP (Ecker, Ticic, Hulley), the LessWrong practical guides, and other clinical methodologies into a single actionable self-help protocol?

## Purpose & Context
This research builds on Finding 2 of the rapid subconscious belief change report, which established that memory reconsolidation is the brain's native mechanism for rapid, enduring belief change. The user now wants a how-to guide — the actual steps to do it on oneself, not just the theory.

### Audience
- Primary: Self-directed individual wanting to change their own subconscious beliefs without a therapist
- Tone: Practical, step-by-step, actionable
- Complexity: Must be clear enough for someone with no therapy background to follow, but grounded in clinical research

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. The RECON protocol (Courtney Armstrong): Recall, Explore, Create mismatch, Objectively describe, Neutralize
2. The Therapeutic Reconsolidation Process / TRP (Ecker et al.): ABC-123-V framework
3. Self-applied reconsolidation techniques from LessWrong (practical guidelines, schema access points)
4. Coherence Therapy methodology
5. EMDR's reconsolidation mechanisms adapted for self-use
6. Practical details: how long each step takes, how to know it's working, what to do when stuck
7. The 4 schema access points: Felt Sense, Belief, Evidence, Metaphor
8. Safety considerations and when NOT to self-apply

### Out of Scope
- Pharmacological methods (propranolol)
- Therapist-only techniques requiring professional facilitation
- General background/theory already covered in the original Finding 2 report

## Required Dimensions
1. **Preparation**: How to identify the target belief/schema to work on
2. **Activation**: How to reactivate the memory/belief to make it labile (optimal arousal level)
3. **Mismatch**: How to generate a genuine prediction error / disconfirming experience
4. **Reconsolidation**: What to do during the labile window (4-6 hours)
5. **Verification**: How to confirm the belief has actually changed
6. **Troubleshooting**: What to do when it doesn't work (resistance, meta-schemas, deactivation)

## Source Requirements
- Primary: RECON protocol sources, Ecker et al. TRP primer, LessWrong practical guidelines
- Supporting: Clinical papers on reconsolidation protocols
- 15-25 sources

## Output
A practical, step-by-step protocol guide titled "How to Change a Subconscious Belief Using Memory Reconsolidation: A Self-Applied Protocol"
