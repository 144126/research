## Research Topic: Self-Applied Memory Reconsolidation Protocol for Subconscious Belief Change

## Integrated Findings

### From RECON Protocol (Courtney Armstrong)
- 5 steps: Recall (briefly), Explore (beliefs attached), Create (mismatch experience), Objectively describe (from detached perspective), Neutralize (repeat until clean)
- Key insight: recall briefly, don't relive; the "mismatch experience" is central
- Window: activate memory, then have 10min-5hrs to insert new learning
- Observational retelling like watching a movie screen
- Worksheets available for embodied beliefs, desired beliefs, future self script

### From TRP / Ecker, Ticic, Hulley (ABC-123-V)
- A: Symptom identification - clarify what to eliminate and when it happens
- B: Retrieval of target learning - bring implicit emotional schema into explicit awareness as felt experience
- C: Identification of disconfirming knowledge - find vivid experience incompatible with target schema
- 1: Reactivate symptom-requiring schema (B)
- 2: Activate disconfirming knowledge (C) - creating mismatch/juxtaposition
- 3: Repetitions of B-C pairing (2-3 times minimum)
- V: Verification - observe non-reactivation, symptom cessation, effortless permanence
- The mismatch must be EXPERIENCED not just intellectually understood
- Juxtaposition: holding both knowings as true simultaneously creates cognitive dissonance that forces neural update
- Counterlearning requires repetitions of the disconfirming juxtaposition

### From LessWrong / Practical Guides (Trinley Goldenberg)
- **Hierarchy of techniques** (least to most challenging): Experience → Question → Challenge → Dissonance → Change
- **4 Schema Access Points**: Felt Sense (physiological), Belief (semantic), Evidence (episodic memories), Metaphor (metaphorical representations)
- **Start with more cognitively fused schema** - avoids confirmation bias
- **Resistance = Meta-schema** - when stuck, there's a higher-order belief that change would be harmful; work on that first
- **Reverse your fusion** - if you fuse with schemas, create distance; if you distance, try fusing
- **Go with most salient access point** - adapt technique to what naturally arises
- **Experience schema as true IS necessary for revision** - don't push it away
- Questioning techniques (Lefkoe, Byron Katie, Sedona) strike optimal balance of challenge and non-judgment
- Acceptance statements from Coherence Therapy: say the belief and its logic out loud

### From Coherence Therapy
- Symptom coherence approach: symptoms make sense given the emotional learning
- Integration phase: inhabit the emotional truth fully
- Transformation phase: juxtaposition of contradictory knowledge
- Between-session tasks for integration
- 3 repetitions of juxtaposition typically sufficient
- Requires active empathy and skilled facilitation for complex trauma

### From EMDR (Self-Applied)
- Butterfly Hug method: cross-arm tapping while focusing on memory
- Open EMDR: online tool for self-administered bilateral stimulation
- Controversial: Shapiro warned against self-application for severe trauma
- May be safe for mild-moderate distress
- Working Memory Theory: bilateral stimulation loads working memory, reducing vividness
- 12 peer-reviewed studies support Butterfly Hug effectiveness

### Self-Directed Timeline Protocol (Clint Matheny)
- 7-step protocol for shame: choose event, find timeline, float above, locate event, go to day before, let nervous system respond, return to now
- Creates prediction error by looking at past event from "before" perspective
- Works in ~10 minutes for many people
- Safe only for tolerable memories

### Verification Markers (from Ecker et al.)
- Non-reactivation: triggers no longer activate emotional response
- Symptom cessation: behaviors/emotions disappear
- Effortless permanence: change requires no maintenance
- Belief feels foreign/absurd when recalled
- Nervous system shows safety signals, not danger signals

### Safety
- NOT for: panic/flashbacks/dissociation/shutdown
- NOT for: physical violence, life-threatening situations, sexual abuse (without professional)
- Must stay within window of tolerance
- Post-session: protect reconsolidation window (4-6 hrs)
- Work with "small" memories first, build capacity

### Troubleshooting
- If schema continues to reactivate: need more repetitions, different component, or different context
- Meta-schemas: protective beliefs resisting change
- Deactivation: challenge too strong, schema goes "underground"
- Resistance = information about what needs work next
