# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What is the best brand name for a chess training web app targeting Nigerian/West African players, where you play Stockfish and get AI coaching that explains every move — with the name needing to feel "smart & capable," be short/memorable/pronounceable, and avoid looking generic or AI-suffixed?

## Purpose & Context
This research is for a founder who built a chess training app (currently internally called "Beee Chess" / "Chess AI" — names they never wanted to use) and needs a real brand name before launch. The app is SvelteKit-based, uses Stockfish in a Web Worker, streams AI coaching via Groq/OpenRouter, and monetizes via Paystack tokens (Nigerian payment gateway). Nigerian chess is growing explosively — school tournaments with 750+ students, corporate sponsorships, a national push to produce the country's first Grandmaster [1][2][3]. The name must resonate with this audience.

The audience (the founder) needs practical, actionable name recommendations — not just a list of random generated names, but a reasoned analysis of what makes a strong name for THIS specific product in THIS market, with trademark/domain considerations and competitive differentiation.

### Audience
- **Primary**: A founder/developer naming their chess app (business-savvy, technically literate)
- **Secondary**: Potentially investors, early users in Nigeria/West Africa
- **Tone**: Direct, evidence-driven, practical — like a naming agency brief
- **Complexity**: Business strategy + linguistics + brand strategy + product positioning
- **Child-reader adaptivity**: Every marketing/branding/linguistic term must include an inline ELI5 explanation [imagine: rephrasing simply enough for a 9-year-old]

### Decision Context
- The app is fully built and working — the name is the last major branding decision before growth
- Domain "chess.apexlinks.org" is staying — the name does NOT need a matching .com
- The name needs to work for Nigerian/West African players primarily (not a global audience first)
- It should feel "smart & capable" — intelligent, competent, strong — not warm/playful, not generic tech
- Must be distinctive from existing chess apps (Chess.com, Lichess, Chessable, DecodeChess, Aimchess, Dr. Wolf, Chessvia/Chessy, CheckmateX, Sensei Chess, Chessiro, Noctie.ai)
- Must avoid "AI" suffix (dated/generic by 2026 standards), avoid "Chess" prefix if possible (too category-generic), avoid names that sound like expired coupon codes (Taskrr, FlowifyAI style)
- Should follow 2026 naming best practices: short (7-12 chars ideally), one word, easy to pronounce after hearing once, easy to spell, works well in verbal recommendation

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. Analysis of naming patterns that resonate with Nigerian/West African audiences — whether English names, African-language-derived names, or international names
2. Inventory of every notable chess app/brand name globally and analysis of why each works or fails
3. Analysis of the "smart & capable" naming vibe — what kinds of names communicate intelligence, competence, strength
4. 2026 naming best practices for apps: length, phonetics, memorability, trademark-ability
5. Competitive whitespace: what naming territory is unoccupied in the chess app space
6. Nigerian tech/brand naming landscape: what kinds of brand names perform well with Nigerian consumers (e.g., Paystack, Flutterwave, Andela, PiggyVest, Kuda, Bamboo, Risevest)
7. Short brandable name generation frameworks: invented words, real words in new contexts, portmanteaus, metaphorical names
8. Trademark risk assessment for name categories (what's too generic, what conflicts with existing marks)
9. Specific name recommendations with rationale for each (minimum 8-10 candidates)
10. How to test and validate name candidates with target users

### Out of Scope
1. Logo/visual identity design
2. App store optimization (ASO) strategy beyond basic naming
3. Full marketing strategy
4. Company/legal entity naming separate from product naming
5. Domain availability (domain stays as-is)
6. Tagline/slogan development

### Timeframe
Primary focus: 2025-2026 naming trends with foundational context from 2020-2024.

### Geographic Focus
Primary: Nigeria/West Africa. Secondary: Global chess app landscape for competitive comparison.

## Required Dimensions of Investigation

Each dimension must be investigated from multiple angles and reported with specific evidence:

1. **Competitive Landscape Analysis**
   - Every chess-related app brand: Chess.com (descriptive compound), Lichess (made-up), Chessable (suffixed), DecodeChess (compound+category), Aimchess (compound), Dr. Wolf (personal name), Chessvia/Chessy (modified word), CheckmateX (compound+letter), Sensei Chess (honorific+category), Chessiro (branded), Noctie.ai (evocative+.ai TLD)
   - How many use "Chess" in the name? Does that help or hurt?
   - What naming territory is completely unoccupied?
   - How do successful non-chess apps in Nigerian/West African market name themselves?

2. **Nigerian/West African Consumer Branding**
   - What brand names resonate with Nigerian players specifically?
   - Analysis of successful Nigerian tech brands: Paystack, Flutterwave, Andela, Kuda, PiggyVest, Bamboo, Risevest, Mono, Busha, Juicyway, Brass, Zitma — what patterns do they follow?
   - What linguistic/cultural considerations matter for a chess app name in Nigeria?
   - How does Tunde Onakoya's Chess in Slums movement and associated messaging (chess as discipline, opportunity, intelligence) inform brand positioning?
   - What Nigerian languages (Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, Pidgin) might contribute name elements — and what are the risks of cultural appropriation or mispronunciation?

3. **Linguistic & Phonetic Analysis of Great Names**
   - Why do one-syllable and two-syllable names dominate top apps? (Slack, Notion, Figma, Linear, Stripe, Calm, Arc, Loom)
   - What phonetic patterns signal "smart & capable"? (Hard consonants like K/T/P, short vowels, crisp endings — e.g., Stripe, Arc, Notion)
   - What makes a name easy to say after hearing it once? (CV-CV syllable structure, familiar phonemes, no consonant clusters)
   - What names in chess-adjacent spaces work well and why? (Gotham Chess, Hikaru, Chessbrah — personal brand names)
   - What are the common failure modes of chess app names? (Generic/category names, hard-to-spell invented words, homophones)

4. **Naming Patterns & Frameworks**
   - Real words in new contexts (Linear, Notion, Slack, Arc, Stripe) — what words are available in "smart & capable" space?
   - Invented words (Lichess, Venmo, Zillow, Hulu) — what phonetic patterns make these work?
   - Modified words (Lyft, Flickr, Chessy) — is this pattern 2026-appropriate or dated?
   - Compound words (CheckmateX, Chessable, Facebook) — does this work for a chess app?
   - Personal/character names (Dr. Wolf, Magnus Trainer) — would this work?
   - Evocative/metaphor names (Apple, Slack, Patagonia) — best fit for "smart & capable"?

5. **"Smart & Capable" Name Vibe Deconstruction**
   - What specific words evoke intelligence? (Apex, Sage, Wit, Keen, Sharp, Bright, Clever, Savvy, Astute, Perceptive, Acute)
   - What words evoke capability/strength? (Able, Capable, Power, Force, Might, Strong, Solid, Steel, Iron, Core)
   - What invented names sound smart? (Lichess sounds light/quick; Perplexity sounds curious/intelligent; Anthropic sounds human+intelligent)
   - What sound symbolism [imagine: how the sounds in a word affect how we feel about it] makes a name feel intelligent? (The /k/ sound in "keen" and "acute" feels sharp; the /s/ sound in "sage" and "savvy" feels wise)
   - Analysis of name endings: -ly (friendly, approachable), -ic (scientific, precise), -o (warm, global), -a (feminine, elegant), -ix (tech, strong), consonant-end (crisp, decisive)

6. **Trademark & Legal Considerations**
   - What chess-related terms are too generic to trademark? (Chess, Check, Checkmate, Pawn, Knight, Bishop, Rook, Queen, King, Board, Match, Game)
   - What existing trademarks exist in the chess app space? (USPTO Class 9 for downloadable software, Class 41 for entertainment services)
   - What naming patterns trigger USPTO refusal? (Mere descriptiveness, confusing similarity)
   - Names to avoid due to major brand proximity (anything with "GPT", "Claude", "Gemini", "Open" prefix, "Chat")

7. **Domain-Standing Naming (Name doesn't need to match domain)**
   - When the domain doesn't need to match, what naming constraints disappear? You can use more invented names, not worry about TLD availability
   - Case studies of apps with non-matching brand names and domains: Notion (notion.so, not .com), Linear (linear.app), Arc (arc.net), Raycast (raycast.com — does match but .com was premium)

8. **Real-World Chess Community Brand Reception**
   - What do chess players on Reddit, Chess.com forums, and Lichess think about various chess app brands?
   - How do Nigerian chess players discover and discuss chess tools? (Word of mouth, WhatsApp groups, Twitter/X, Instagram)
   - What naming mistakes have other chess apps made that turned off users?
   - User-reported sentiment on existing chess AI coaching tools

9. **Name Candidate Generation & Analysis**
   - Generate 15-20 specific name candidates across different naming patterns
   - For each candidate: phonetic analysis, meaning, why it fits "smart & capable", why it works for Nigerian audience, why it's distinctive from competitors, potential issues
   - Rank top 5-8 candidates with detailed rationale

10. **Validation Frameworks**
    - How to test name candidates with Nigerian players specifically (not general population)
    - What metrics matter for name validation? (Memorability, pronounceability, emotional response, category fit, distinctiveness)
    - Specific testing methodology: guerrilla testing via WhatsApp groups, Twitter/X polls, in-person at chess clubs

11. **Public Opinion, User Sentiment & Social Proof**
    - What do real Nigerian chess players and West African chess communities say about chess training apps and tools — what do they want, what's missing?
    - What names do they naturally gravitate toward?
    - What do existing chess app users complain about regarding branding?
    - User-reported (not vendor) perspectives on what makes a chess app trustworthy and appealing in West Africa

## Output Requirements

### Report Structure
1. **Executive Summary** (1000-1500 words) — synthesize ALL findings: competitive whitespace, Nigerian consumer naming patterns, "smart & capable" phonetic analysis, top 8 name candidates ranked, and validation recommendations
2. **Introduction** — the app, why it needs a name, the audience, methodology, assumptions
3. **Main Analysis** (8-12 findings):
   - Finding 1: The Chess App Name Landscape — What's Taken, What's Open
   - Finding 2: How Nigerian Consumers Respond to Brand Names (Patterns from Paystack, Kuda, Flutterwave, etc.)
   - Finding 3: Deconstructing "Smart & Capable" — Phonetics, Semantics, Sound Symbolism
   - Finding 4: Naming Patterns That Work in 2026 (Real Words, Invented, Modified, Compound)
   - Finding 5: What to Avoid — Generic Terms, AI Suffixes, Trademark Traps
   - Finding 6: Name Candidates (15-20 generated, 5-8 deeply analyzed)
   - Finding 7: Top Name Recommendation with Full Rationale
   - Finding 8: Runner-Up Candidates with Trade-offs
   - Finding 9: Validation Methodology for Nigerian Audience
   - Finding 10: Implementation Next Steps (after name selection)
4. **Synthesis & Insights** — cross-cutting patterns, novel connections
5. **Limitations & Caveats** — gaps, uncertainties, what wasn't found
6. **Recommendations** — the specific name(s) to use, in order of priority
7. **Complete Bibliography** — every citation [1]-[N], no placeholders
8. **Methodology Appendix** — research process, verification, confidence levels

### Quality Mandates
- EVERY factual claim followed by [N] citation in the same sentence
- No vague attributions ("research shows", "experts believe", "studies suggest")
- Distinguish facts FROM sources vs. your own synthesis explicitly
- For speculative content, label as "This suggests..." not "Research shows..."
- Minimum 3 independent sources per major claim
- Prose >=80%, bullets sparingly (only for distinct lists like name candidates)
- No placeholder text, no "content continues", no ranges in bibliography
- Admit uncertainty honestly
- **Distinguish evidence classes explicitly**: label every claim as 'vendor-sourced' (marketing claims from companies), 'user-reported' (chess players, forum posts, social media), or 'expert/third-party' (branding experts, analysts, academic research)
- **End with a "Weakest Evidence" section** listing the 3 claims least well-supported

### Bias Safeguards
- Actively seek Nigerian-specific sources — don't default to US-centric naming advice
- Flag when naming recommendations favor Western tastes over Nigerian audience fit
- Note when research is sparse (e.g., limited data on Nigerian chess player brand preferences)
- Distinguish between evidence from naming agencies (who sell naming services) and independent research
- Be honest about what's personal opinion/subjective vs. evidence-based
