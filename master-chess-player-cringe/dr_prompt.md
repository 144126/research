# Deep Research Commission - Exact Prompt

## Research Question
Why does the name "Master Chess Player" (suggested by a 58-year-old woman for an AI chess coach app targeting young adults) sound "cringe" to a 24-year-old? What are the specific cultural, linguistic, generational, and branding mechanisms at play?

## Purpose & Context
This research explains a concrete generational naming collision. A 58-year-old Boomer woman proposed "Master Chess Player" as the name for an AI chess coaching app. A 24-year-old Gen Z user finds it unappealing/cringe. The research must explain WHY — identifying every mechanism: semantic baggage of "master," generational naming preferences, Gen Z's cringe detector, chess culture differences, and what would work instead.

- Primary: Product founders, naming strategists, marketers building AI consumer apps
- Secondary: Anyone designing for cross-generational appeal
- Tone: Analytical, evidence-driven, precise
- Complexity: Accessible to non-specialists; [imagine: ...] for 9-year-old on hard terms

### Decision Context
- What naming strategy should an AI chess coach app use for a Gen Z target audience?
- What went wrong in the 58-year-old's naming intuition, and what generational gap does it reveal?
- How should founders navigate Boomer → Gen Z naming disagreements?

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. The specific linguistic and cultural baggage of the word "master" in 2026 American English
2. Gen Z brand naming preferences vs Boomer naming preferences (empirical data)
3. The "cringe" mechanism — how Gen Z detects and rejects inauthentic/forced branding
4. Chess culture differences: how older chess enthusiasts vs Gen Z chess players talk about the game
5. The specific semantic profile of "Master Chess Player" — sound symbolism, length, formality, descriptive-ness
6. What naming patterns actually work for AI consumer apps targeting Gen Z
7. The "self-aggrandizement" taboo in Gen Z culture (self-applied honorifics = cringe)
8. Real-world precedents: terms like "master bedroom," "master sommelier," "master branch" being retired

### Out of Scope
- General AI ethics or chess engine technical comparisons
- Full brand strategy beyond naming
- Non-English naming considerations
- Monetization or pricing strategy
