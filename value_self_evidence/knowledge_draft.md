# Research Topic: The "Value Self-Evidence" Thesis

## Research Question
When does genuine value become self-evident to potential buyers, and when does it require persuasion or marketing to be recognized? Is the claim "if it truly has value, people will see it and desire it automatically" empirically supported, or does it conflate different types of value, different decision contexts, and different cognitive mechanisms?

## Initial Scope

### In Scope
- Evidence for/against self-evident value
- Conditions where persuasion is vs. isn't necessary
- Mechanisms of value recognition (cognitive, emotional, social)
- Relationship between product quality and marketing effectiveness
- Ethical boundaries of persuasion
- Real-world case studies of "value speaks for itself" vs. "value needs framing"

### Out of Scope
- General business strategy advice
- Specific marketing tactics or playbooks
- Industry-specific how-to guides
- Sales training methodologies
