# Phase 1: SCOPE - Research Framing

## Decomposed Research Question
The core claim: "If something truly has value, people will see it and desire it automatically — no need to persuade them."

### Sub-Questions
1. What is "value" — functional, perceived, signaled, experiential?
2. What is "self-evidence" — property of thing, observer, or context?
3. When do people accurately recognize value without persuasion?
4. When do people fail to recognize value even when it exists?
5. What cognitive, emotional, and social mechanisms govern value recognition?
6. What is the empirical relationship between product quality and marketing effectiveness?
7. What incentives distort evidence on either side?
8. Is the "authenticity" movement empirically grounded?

## Key Assumptions to Validate
1. That value can be objectively measured vs. purely constructed
2. That marketing effectiveness inversely correlates with product quality
3. That consumers are rational evaluators of quality
4. That "persuasion" and "demonstration" are distinct categories
5. That historical data can inform current digital marketplace dynamics

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources with balanced perspectives
- Evidence-backed resolution of the core thesis
- Clear conditions under which value is/isn't self-evident
- Practical implications for founder decision-making

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- **Pro-self-evidence**: founders with limited budgets, anti-marketing purists, PLG advocates
- **Pro-persuasion**: marketing agencies, ad platforms, persuasion consultants
- **Integration school**: balanced academics, experienced practitioners

# Phase 2: PLAN - Research Strategy

## Priority Search Paths
1. Academic: consumer psychology, behavioral economics of value perception
2. Industry: marketing science (Ehrenberg-Bass, Nielsen, Kantar)
3. Case studies: Patagonia, Trader Joe's, Apple, D2C brands
4. User-reported: Reddit (r/marketing, r/startups, r/entrepreneur), reviews
5. Contrarian: critiques of both authenticity marketing and persuasion marketing
6. Quantitative: ad blocker stats, trust barometers, marketing ROI studies
7. Historical: evolution of marketing philosophy from pre-industrial to present
8. Future: AI, attention scarcity, authenticity in deepfake era

## Query Variants per Dimension
- Core: "value self-evident" "intrinsic value recognition" "better mousetrap fallacy"
- Technical: "perceived value psychology" "value construction" "functional vs signaled value"
- Recent: "2024 2025 authentic marketing ROI" "product-led growth evidence"
- Academic: "consumer value perception" "persuasion knowledge model" "persuasion resistance"
- Contrarian: "critique authentic marketing" "persuasion is manipulation"
- Data: "ad avoidance statistics 2024" "consumer trust in advertising 2025"
- Industry: "marketing vs product quality correlation" "brand authenticity measurement"
- User: "reddit marketing persuasion authenticity" "consumer skepticism advertising"
