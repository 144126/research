# Phase 1: SCOPE

## Research Question
Is it safe to stop masturbating midway (i.e., edging, stop-start technique, orgasm control)? What are the documented medical effects, risks, and benefits?

## Core Components
1. **Safety of edging/stop-start technique** - acute and chronic effects
2. **Epididymal hypertension ("blue balls")** - mechanisms, risks, resolution
3. **Pelvic floor consequences** - tension, dysfunction, pain
4. **Delayed ejaculation risk** - conditioned behavioral effects
5. **Prostate health** - congestion, cancer risk correlations
6. **Neurochemical effects** - dopamine, oxytocin, prolactin
7. **Psychological aspects** - compulsion, NoFap, semen retention communities
8. **Therapeutic applications** - premature ejaculation treatment

## In Scope
- Peer-reviewed medical research on edging, stop-start technique, epididymal hypertension
- Clinical guidelines from urology associations (Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, etc.)
- Government health sources (MedlinePlus, NIH, CDC)
- Sexual health organizations (Planned Parenthood, SMSNA)
- Pelvic floor physical therapy literature
- Neuroscience of orgasm and arousal
- Personal/user-reported experiences from forums and communities

## Out of Scope
- Moral/religious arguments about masturbation
- Non-evidence-based semen retention claims without medical backing
- Pornography addiction debates specifically
- Fertility and conception topics
- Pediatric/adolescent sexual development

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources with average credibility >75/100
- Minimum 4 source types represented
- All core components addressed with 3+ independent sources per major claim
- Executive summary with inline citations
- Complete bibliography (no placeholders)

## Key Assumptions to Validate
- Edging is widely practiced and has been studied formally
- Stop-start technique is a validated medical treatment
- "Blue balls" is a real but harmless physiological phenomenon
- Chronic edging may differ from occasional use in risk profile
