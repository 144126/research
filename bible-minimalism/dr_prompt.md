# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What does the Bible — across the Old and New Testaments, intertestamental literature, and the early church tradition — actually teach about minimalism, simplicity, material possessions, wealth, and poverty? How has this teaching been interpreted across Christian history (patristic, medieval, monastic, Reformation, modern), what are the areas of consensus and disagreement, and how should a 21st-century Christian discern a faithful approach to material simplicity?

## Purpose & Context
This research serves a dual audience: a theologically engaged adult reader and a 9-year-old child who will also read the report. The topic matters because minimalism has become a major cultural movement in the 2020s, and many Christians are asking whether it aligns with — or distracts from — biblical faithfulness. Secular minimalism (Joshua Fields Millburn, Ryan Nicodemus, Marie Kondo, Fumio Sasaki) has generated tens of millions of adherents worldwide, but its philosophical foundations (individualism, self-actualization, autonomy) differ sharply from Christian theology (creaturely dependence, communal obligation, worship of God). At the same time, the Bible is filled with warnings about wealth and calls to simplicity. The tension between "the Bible is basically minimalist" and "minimalism is a competing religion" needs careful, source-grounded resolution.

### Audience
- Primary: Theologically literate adults seeking a definitive biblical framework for evaluating minimalism as a lifestyle philosophy
- Secondary: A 9-year-old child who needs every hard concept explained inline
- Tone: Formal and precise for the adult, but with constant inline [imagine: ...] simplifications so the child never gets lost
- Complexity: Academic-depth exegesis of key passages, historical survey of interpretive traditions, and practical wisdom — while remaining accessible
- Child-reader adaptivity: Every term like "eschatology," "asceticism," "stewardship," "covenant," "eschatological," "teleological," "patristic," "hermeneutical," "anthropology," "soteriological" must be followed immediately by an inline [imagine: ...] explanation in the simplest possible words

### Decision Context
- What decision will this research inform? Christians deciding how to relate to the minimalist movement — whether to embrace it, reject it, adapt it, or build a distinctively Christian alternative
- What are the stakes? At stake is whether millions of Christians adopt a lifestyle philosophy that may either deepen or undermine their faith; also at stake is the church's witness on consumerism, generosity, and ecological stewardship
- What would change if the answer is X vs Y? If minimalism is biblically endorsed, churches should promote it; if it is a competing religion, churches should warn against it; if it is a neutral tool, churches should teach discernment

## Research Scope

### In Scope
1. Exegesis of all major biblical passages about wealth, possessions, simplicity, contentment, and greed — including but not limited to: Matthew 6:19-34, Luke 12:13-34, Luke 16:1-31, Luke 18:18-30, 1 Timothy 6:3-19, Hebrews 13:5, Philippians 4:10-13, Proverbs 30:7-9, Ecclesiastes 5:10-20, Deuteronomy 8:11-18, Psalm 49, Psalm 37, Job 1-2, 42, Acts 2:42-47, Acts 4:32-37, 2 Corinthians 8-9, James 2:1-7, James 5:1-6, Mark 10:17-31, Mark 12:41-44, and the Sermon on the Mount/Plain
2. The economic context of first-century Galilee and Judea (what "rich" and "poor" actually meant)
3. The historical Jesus's own economic status and that of his first disciples
4. Old Testament wisdom literature's nuanced view of wealth as both blessing and danger
5. The Hebrew Bible's treatment of prosperity in the Deuteronomic covenant vs. the prophetic critique of wealth
6. The early church fathers on wealth, poverty, and simplicity (Clement of Alexandria, "Who Is the Rich Man That Shall Be Saved?"; John Chrysostom; Basil of Caesarea; Augustine; Ambrose)
7. The monastic tradition and its vow of poverty (Anthony, Pachomius, Benedict, Francis of Assisi)
8. The Reformation's re-evaluation of wealth and vocation (Luther, Calvin, the Puritans)
9. Modern Christian responses to the minimalist movement (2000-2026)
10. The Christian simplicity movement (Richard Foster, Dorothy Day, Mennonite/Anabaptist traditions)

### Out of Scope
1. Secular minimalism as a standalone topic (only examined in relation to biblical teaching)
2. The prosperity gospel (only referenced as a contrast case)
3. Interior design minimalism as an aesthetic
4. Non-Christian religious traditions on simplicity (Buddhism, Hinduism, etc.)
5. Detailed economic policy recommendations

### Timeframe
Foundational sources: biblical texts (c. 1400 BC - AD 100), patristic sources (AD 100-500), medieval monastic sources (AD 500-1500), Reformation sources (AD 1500-1700), modern Christian responses (AD 1800-2026). Emphasis on recent scholarship (2000-2026) and the current minimalist movement (2010-2026).

### Geographic Focus
Global Christian perspective, with emphasis on Western Christianity (where minimalism has been most influential) but including Global South voices and non-Western Christian perspectives on wealth and simplicity.

## Required Dimensions of Investigation

Each dimension must be investigated from multiple angles and reported with specific evidence, inline citations [N], and evidence class labels (expert/third-party, user-reported, or vendor-sourced — though in this topic "vendor-sourced" is less applicable):

1. **Biblical Exegesis (Technical/Mechanistic)**
   - What does each major passage teach about material possessions in its original context?
   - How do the Gospels differ in their economic emphasis (Mark's bluntness, Matthew's community focus, Luke's radical concern for the poor, John's theological framing)?
   - What is the relationship between the Deuteronomic prosperity promises (blessing for obedience) and the prophetic/wisdom critiques of wealth?
   - How do the Pauline and pastoral epistles handle wealth (especially 1 Timothy 6, 2 Corinthians 8-9)?
   - What is the significance of Jesus's own economic status and lifestyle?

2. **Historical Context & Evolution**
   - What was the economic reality of first-century Galilee? Were Jesus and his disciples poor, middle-class, or something else?
   - How did the early church (Acts 2-4) practice radical sharing, and was it normative or descriptive?
   - How did the church fathers interpret Jesus's commands about wealth — literally or metaphorically?
   - How did monasticism develop the ideal of voluntary poverty from biblical roots?

3. **Current State-of-Art**
   - What are the major Christian books and resources on simplicity and minimalism published 2000-2026?
   - What is the state of academic biblical scholarship on wealth and poverty (e.g., Brueggemann, Hays, Longenecker, Friesen, Oakman, Schmidt, Rhee)?
   - How are contemporary Christians actually practicing simplicity — what movements, networks, and experiments exist?

4. **Quantitative Evidence**
   - What percentage of Jesus's teaching concerns money and possessions (estimates range from 11-25% of the Synoptic Gospels)?
   - How many times does the Bible address wealth, poverty, and possessions?
   - What data exists on Christian adoption of minimalist practices?
   - What survey data exists on Christian attitudes toward wealth, giving, and simplicity?

5. **Stakeholder Analysis**
   - Who are the major voices advocating Christian minimalism (Becca Ehrlich, Daniel Pentimone, Cheryl at BiblicalMinimalism.com, Regina Walton)?
   - Who are the major critics and what are their arguments (Megan Hill, Caleb Levi, Derek Brown, Mere Orthodoxy)?
   - What do mainstream evangelical and Catholic authorities say?
   - How do Global South Christians view Western minimalist trends?

6. **Competing Approaches Comparison**
   - How does Christian minimalism differ from secular minimalism at the philosophical and theological level?
   - How does the Christian simplicity tradition (Foster, Longacre, the Anabaptists) differ from the newer "Christian minimalism" movement?
   - How do Catholic social teaching, Protestant stewardship theology, and Eastern Orthodox ascetic tradition compare on possessions?
   - How does prosperity theology contrast with simplicity theology?

7. **Criticisms & Limitations**
   - What are the theological dangers of minimalism: works-righteousness, self-righteousness, neglect of neighbor, avoidance of material blessings?
   - Is minimalism compatible with biblical hospitality, which often requires abundance?
   - Does minimalism reduce Christianity to a lifestyle brand?
   - Does the minimalist focus on personal decluttering distract from systemic justice?

8. **Contrarian & Heterodox Perspectives**
   - Could it be argued that the Bible actually endorses wealth as a sign of God's blessing (Deuteronomy 28, Proverbs 10:22, Job 42)?
   - Is "Christian minimalism" a misreading that imposes a modern Western aesthetic on ancient texts?
   - Does the Bible celebrate abundance, feasting, and beauty in ways that contradict minimalism?
   - Were the wealthiest biblical figures (Abraham, Solomon, Job) actually models of faithfulness?

9. **Future Trajectories & Predictions**
   - Where is the Christian simplicity movement heading?
   - How might climate change and ecological concerns reshape Christian thinking about possessions?
   - Will Christian minimalism grow as a response to late-stage capitalism, or fade as a trend?

10. **Regulatory, Legal & Ethical Dimensions**
    - What are the ethical obligations of wealthy Christians toward the poor?
    - How does the biblical tithe relate to minimalist giving?
    - What is the role of the local church in discipling Christians about possessions?

11. **Geographic & Geopolitical Variation**
    - How do Christians in the Global South (Africa, Latin America, Asia) view Western Christian minimalism?
    - How does the Bible's teaching on wealth read differently in contexts of extreme poverty vs. affluence?
    - What can the Western church learn from persecuted and poor churches about simplicity?

12. **Practical Applications & Case Studies**
    - What does faithful Christian simplicity look like in practice?
    - Case studies of Christians who have adopted minimalist practices for kingdom purposes
    - How should a local church teach about money and simplicity without falling into legalism?

13. **Public Opinion, User Sentiment & Social Proof**
    - What do real Christians (not theologians or pastors) say about minimalism in online communities, forums, social media, and reviews?
    - What do practitioners of Christian minimalism report as its benefits and drawbacks?
    - What do critics report as concerns or negative experiences?
    - What is the consensus vs. divergence across different Christian traditions (evangelical, Catholic, Orthodox, mainline Protestant)?
