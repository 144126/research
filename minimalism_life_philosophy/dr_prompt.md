# Deep Research Prompt: Minimalism as a Life Philosophy

## Research Question
What is the evidence for minimalism as a life philosophy — its origins, psychological effects, criticisms, and practical outcomes? Does owning less and living more simply actually make people happier, or is it a privileged aesthetic for the affluent?

## Audience
A curious general reader (academic background, but wants to share findings with a 9-year-old). Purpose is general exploration and understanding.

## Scope Boundaries
**In:** Minimalism as a lifestyle movement (not visual art/design aesthetics). Voluntary simplicity, decluttering (Marie Kondo, The Minimalists). Psychological research on materialism vs. well-being. Historical/philosophical roots (Stoicism, Zen Buddhism, Thoreau, Epicureanism). Criticisms (privilege critique, commercialized minimalism, cultural appropriation). Practical outcomes (financial, mental health, environmental). User-reported experiences from online communities.

**Out:** Product/UI/UX design. Software architecture. Graphic design. Architectural minimalism as an art movement.

## Dimensions (13 required)
1. Technical/Definitional
2. Historical
3. State-of-art
4. Quantitative
5. Stakeholders
6. Competing approaches
7. Criticisms
8. Contrarian
9. Future
10. Regulatory/Ethical
11. Geographic
12. Practical
13. Public opinion / user sentiment

## Source Requirements
- Minimum 270 sources
- At least 4 source types (academic, industry, news, user-reported, books)
- Mix of recent (2022-2026) and foundational (Thoreau 1854, Kondo 2011, etc.)
- Both supportive and critical perspectives
- Geographic diversity (US, Japan, Scandinavia, Europe, Global South)
- User-reported sources required per section

## Timeframe
Historical roots to present day (2026).

## Geography
Global, with emphasis on US/Japan/Scandinavia.
