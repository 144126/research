## Phase 1: Scope Document

### Core Decomposition
1. **Definitional Boundaries:** What distinguishes lifestyle minimalism from voluntary simplicity, frugality, essentialism? Competing definitions.
2. **Historical Evolution:** Thoreau → 1960s counterculture → 1990s simplicity circles → 2010s decluttering boom → 2020s digital minimalism.
3. **Philosophical Roots:** Stoicism (Seneca, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius), Zen Buddhism (wabi-sabi, danshari), Epicureanism, Transcendentalism.
4. **Psychological Evidence:** Materialism ↔ well-being literature. Clutter ↔ cortisol. Decision fatigue. Choice architecture.
5. **Stakeholder Analysis:** Key figures, publishers, corporations, influencers.
6. **Criticisms & Contrarian Views:** Privilege critique, commercialized minimalism, cultural appropriation, rebound effects.
7. **Practical Outcomes:** Financial, environmental, social, mental health.
8. **Future Trajectory:** Climate anxiety, AI digital minimalism, generational attitudes.
9. **Geographic Variations:** US, Japan, Scandinavia, Europe, Global South.
10. **User Sentiment:** Online community debates, first-person accounts.

### Key Assumptions to Validate
- Minimalism increases happiness/well-being.
- Minimalism requires financial privilege.
- The movement has been co-opted by consumer capitalism.
- Decluttering has lasting vs. rebound effects.
- Minimalism is culturally Western despite Eastern philosophical roots.
