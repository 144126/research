# DEEP RESEARCH COMMISSION

## Research Question
What is the comprehensive landscape of the "ease vs speed" trade-off across software engineering, programming languages, product design, and organizational practices — and how should technical decision-makers navigate this trade-off in 2026?

## Purpose & Context
This research informs a technology decision-maker who is evaluating tooling, language, framework, and process choices for upcoming projects. They need to understand when optimizing for ease (developer productivity, rapid iteration, low cognitive load) is the right call vs when optimizing for speed (runtime performance, execution efficiency, throughput) dominates. The audience has deep technical background but needs a framework for making these trade-off decisions systematically rather than by intuition.

## Scope
### In Scope
1. Programming language trade-offs: ease of development vs execution speed
2. Product/UX design trade-offs: ease of use vs system performance
3. Software engineering trade-offs: development velocity vs code quality
4. Developer experience vs user experience conflicts
5. Economic analysis: developer time costs vs compute time costs
6. Organizational trade-offs: team productivity, hiring, onboarding
7. Frameworks and decision models (ATAM, RPEM, etc.)
8. Impact of AI/LLMs on the trade-off (2024-2026)

### Out of Scope
- Hardware-level performance optimization
- Specific database performance comparisons
- Network protocol optimization
- Gaming, HFT, embedded systems (except as examples)

### Timeframe
2020-2026 with emphasis on 2024-2026

## Required Source Count
270+ minimum
