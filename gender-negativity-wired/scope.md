# Phase 1: SCOPE

## Research Question
Are women biologically/evolutionarily "wired" to perceive their circumstances as worse than they objectively are, or are observed gender differences better explained by social/cultural factors?

## Core Components
1. Evolutionary pressures → sex-differentiated cognitive biases
2. Neural circuitry differences (amygdala, PFC, connectivity)
3. Cognitive patterns (rumination, pessimism, self-assessment)
4. Socialization and cultural influences
5. Methodological issues in sex-difference research

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- Evolutionary psychologists: adaptive design view
- Clinical researchers: depression/anxiety vulnerability
- Feminist psychologists: social construction view
- Neuroscientists: brain-based sex differences

## Scope Boundaries
IN: Human psychological/cognitive/neural sex differences 1970-2026
OUT: Non-human studies, political advocacy, treatment protocols

## Assumptions to Validate
1. Sex differences in negativity bias exist (effect size? robustness?)
2. These differences have a biological component (neural/hormonal)
3. Social factors modulate biological predispositions
4. The "wired" framing has scientific legitimacy vs being metaphorical
