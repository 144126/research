# Chess App Name — Final Recommendation

## Why "Beee Chess" Must Change

- **Childish connotation drives away your target audience.** "Beee" reads as a children's spelling of "bee" (the insect), directly contradicting the premium, sophisticated coaching experience for 800-2000 ELO players. In a market where Dr. Wolf and Nadia signal serious coaching, "Beee" signals kids' game.
- **Zero semantic weight.** The name communicates nothing about chess, coaching, AI, or Socratic learning. Users searching for "chess coach" or "AI chess analysis" will never find "Beee" — it has no discoverability.
- **Phonetically confusing in verbal referral.** "Beee Chess" sounds like "B Chess" or "be Chess" in conversation. In Nigerian Pidgin, "bee" has no chess or coaching resonance. Word-of-mouth referral — the primary growth channel — is crippled.
- **No brand extension path.** If the product evolves beyond its current scope, "Beee" cannot grow with it. Compare: Cursor could never have been called "Code Bee."
- **Competitive irrelevance.** Every serious competitor has a name that signals value (Dr. Wolf = expertise, DecodeChess = analysis, WhyThisMove = understanding). "Beee" signals nothing.

## Top 3 Name Recommendations

### 1. Sage (Primary Recommendation)

A name that communicates wisdom, Socratic teaching, and strategic insight. "Sage" directly maps to the app's core differentiator: not just telling you the best move, but teaching you the *why* behind it. At 1 syllable and 4 letters, it is maximally memorable, globally pronounceable (including in Nigerian English and Pidgin), and sits in an uncontested naming space — no competitor occupies "wisdom." The warm cream/coral/dark design palette perfectly complements the "ancient teacher" aesthetic. Domain `sagemoves.com` is available. No trademark conflicts in coaching Class 41.

### 2. Outpost (Strong Alternative)

A pure chess term that doubles as a general leadership concept — a well-defended square in enemy territory from which you mount your attack. "Outpost" signals serious chess, strategic depth, and the idea of establishing a foothold in your chess improvement journey. It is 2 syllables, has no trademark conflicts, and domain `outpostchess.com` is available. The name works particularly well for competitive players who identify with offensive, positional chess.

### 3. Mirror (Warm Alternative)

Names the product after its core function: showing players a reflection of their true chess selves. The Socratic ideal of a "mirror for the mind" aligns perfectly with the app's coaching philosophy. "Mirror" feels introspective, warm, and emotionally resonant — fitting the gentle cream/coral palette. Domain `mirrorchess.com` is available. The main risk is moderate trademark competition in Class 9.

## Final Recommendation: Sage

**Sage** is the optimal name for this product because it directly communicates the Socratic coaching differentiator — the "why behind moves" — in a single syllable. It is distinctive in a market crowded with person-names (Dr. Wolf, Nadia, Chessy) and descriptive-names (DecodeChess, WhyThisMove), sits in the uncontested "wisdom" naming space, is globally pronounceable including in Nigerian English, has clear domain availability via `sagemoves.com`, low trademark risk, and design compatibility with the warm cream/coral/dark palette.

**Full product name:** Sage Moves ("Sage" for short)
**Primary domain:** sagemoves.com
**Social handles:** @sagemoves (X, Instagram)
