# Phase 1: SCOPE — Research Framing

## Research Question
What are the most effective evidence-based methods for rapidly changing deep-seated beliefs (including subconscious/limiting beliefs) using ONLY psychological, cognitive, behavioral, and experiential techniques — explicitly excluding any chemicals, drugs, medications, supplements, or physical equipment/devices?

## Core Components
1. **Cognitive methods**: CBT, REBT, cognitive restructuring, cognitive defusion, metacognitive therapy
2. **Behavioral methods**: behavioral experiments, exposure therapy, behavioral activation, habit reversal, implementation intentions
3. **Experiential/somatic methods**: mindfulness, body scanning, somatic experiencing, focusing, breathing techniques (pranayama, holotropic breathwork), interoceptive exposure
4. **Memory reconsolidation protocols (drug-free)**: coherence therapy, reconsolidation therapy, RTM, self-directed BLS via tapping/eye movements
5. **NLP techniques**: reframing, anchoring, swish pattern, submodality shifts, timeline therapy, reimprinting
6. **Hypnosis/self-hypnosis**: verbal/mental only, no devices
7. **Written/verbal methods**: journaling, expressive writing, affirmations, gratitude practices, self-persuasion
8. **Social/relational methods**: psychological flexibility, perspective-taking, role-play, imaginal dialogues, empty chair
9. **Meditative/contemplative methods**: loving-kindness, compassion, insight, non-dual, mantra
10. **Identity-based methods**: growth mindset, identity-based habit change, self-concept revision, narrative identity

## Stakeholder Perspectives
- Self-directed individuals seeking to change own beliefs
- Therapists/coaches in low-resource settings
- Academic researchers studying belief change mechanisms
- Contemplative practitioners (Buddhist, yogic, Stoic)
- Skeptics and critics of non-pharmacological methods
- Commercial self-help industry

## Scope Boundaries
**In Scope**: All psychological/behavioral/experiential methods requiring only mind and body
**Out of Scope**: Any pharmacological substances, any physical equipment/devices, supplements, animal studies, methods requiring professional licensing as prerequisite

## Key Assumptions
- Belief change is measurable via implicit/explicit measures
- Drug-free methods can engage the same neural machinery (prediction error, reconsolidation) as drug-assisted methods
- Self-directed change is possible for at least some belief types
- Source availability: 270+ sources across academic, clinical, practitioner, user-reported, and contemplative traditions

## Success Criteria
- 270+ sources with mix of academic, clinical, practitioner, user-reported
- Effect sizes for each major method category
- Time-to-change curves
- Drug-free vs drug-assisted comparison
- Practical protocols for self-directed use
- Weakest evidence self-audit
- Complete bibliography with no placeholders
