# Deep Research Prompt

## Research Topic
Why do people tend to underestimate the intelligence of others — thinking others are "stupid" when they're actually not?

## User's Original Question
"why do i thinking people are stupid, well more stupid than they actually might be"

## Research Dimensions
1. Cognitive biases causing underestimation of others' intelligence (illusory superiority, Dunning-Kruger effect, curse of knowledge, fundamental attribution error, naive cynicism, spotlight effect, outcome bias, egocentric bias, false consensus effect)
2. The Dunning-Kruger effect — how the unskilled overestimate themselves AND how the skilled may overestimate others
3. The curse of knowledge — how knowing something makes it hard to imagine not knowing it, leading to underestimating others' ability to understand
4. Fundamental attribution error — attributing others' mistakes to disposition (stupidity) rather than situation
5. Illusory superiority / above-average effect — most people think they're above average in intelligence
6. Social and cultural factors — competition culture, echo chambers, media amplification of stupid behavior
7. Evolutionary explanations — error management theory, survival over accuracy
8. Metacognitive failures — people are poor at assessing what others know
9. Practical implications — in relationships, work, education, politics
10. How to counteract these biases — debiasing strategies, intellectual humility

- Full research report with 270+ sources
- Evidence-tagged claims (vendor-sourced, user-reported, expert/third-party)
- Executive summary (1000-1500 words)
- Main analysis with 100+ findings
- Synthesis & insights
- Limitations & caveats
- Recommendations
- Complete bibliography
- Methodology appendix
- Weakest evidence self-audit
