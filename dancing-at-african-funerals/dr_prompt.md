## Research Question

Why is dancing an integral part of African funeral traditions? What are the cultural, spiritual, psychological, and social reasons that explain the centrality of dance in African funerary practices?

## Audience & Purpose

General reader with academic curiosity. The report informs understanding of African cultural practices for students, researchers, and anyone seeking to understand why "Africans dance at funerals" — a question that often arises from cross-cultural observation.

## Scope

In: Spiritual/religious functions, psychological/grief-management functions, social cohesion/identity functions, cultural transmission functions, gender dimensions, regional variation (West, East, Southern Africa), contemporary transformations
Out: Diachronic historical reconstruction of origins, North Africa (predominantly Arab/Islamic traditions), detailed musicological analysis of specific rhythms, comparative analysis with non-African traditions (except where illuminating)
Timeframe: Mid-20th century to 2026
Geography: Sub-Saharan Africa (primary: Ghana, Nigeria, Kenya, Malawi, Zimbabwe, South Africa, Mali, Madagascar)

## Dimensions (all 13 required)

1. **Spiritual/theological**: How does ancestral belief drive funeral dancing? What is the cosmology? (Mbiti, van Gennep)
2. **Historical**: How did these practices develop? Early ethnographic accounts. Colonial influences.
3. **State-of-art**: Contemporary research (2020-2026) on funeral dancing. Current academic debates.
4. **Quantitative**: Incidence of funeral dancing across cultures. Psychological measures. TMT studies.
5. **Stakeholders**: Dancers, bereaved families, chiefs/elders, religious authorities, women vs men, youth.
6. **Competing approaches**: Christian/Islamic perspectives vs traditional. Modernization vs preservation.
7. **Criticisms**: Waste of resources? Perpetuation of gender inequality? Resistance from religious groups.
8. **Contrarian**: Is funeral dancing actually declining? Is the "celebration" narrative over-romanticized? Are there communities where it doesn't happen?
9. **Future**: Under pressure from urbanization and religious change. Will it survive? How is it adapting?
10. **Regulatory**: Government policies on traditional practices. UNESCO recognition. Funeral costs regulation.
11. **Geographic**: Variation across West, East, Southern Africa. Notable case studies (Ghana, Kenya, Mali).
12. **Practical**: How are funeral dances organized? Who pays? How long do they last? What skills are needed?
13. **Public opinion / user sentiment**: What do Africans themselves say about funeral dancing? Personal stories, Reddit threads, social media, blogs.
